Warhammer: The Old World - Rise to Glory: Preparations Before the Storm

April 24th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Following on from last time out where I spoke about why I wanted to run this event and what I wanted to try and do different, this time I'm going to go through some of the things which I think will make it special and some of the challenges I've had to overcome.

The Market Phase

This is one of the things I'm most excited for and can't wait to see how it plays out. For those of you who've ever played the Spartacus, Blood and Treachery board game I'm going to be using a similar blind bidding system to represent what new items have been found during the previous rounds games and which of the three teams get their hands on what. I fully expect to have to play this a little on the fly over the weekend with the amount of items available but with (at the time of writing) 64 magic items and 11 Merc Units/Characters up for grabs split between the 18 players I'm fairly sure we'll get to a place where everyone gets some rewards over the weekend.

As mentioned last time, teams will earn Gems for the amount of Victory points they earn in games (each 100VP = 1 Gem) and those Gems can then be spent on acquiring both Magic Items and Mercenary units, these may not remain with players as after each market phase these are then added back to the general pool of rewards and if they're drawn again in a subsequent market phase then the mercenaries have found new employers or the items turned out to be take and a new one has been found.

At the start of each market phase the faction leaders will get to ask for three (this number is the bit I may have to be flexible on) cards, in secret from each other, from either the magic item or mercenary pile to be placed face down on the table in secret, these are then shuffled and laid out top to bottom, other than knowing they've selected X amount of one pile and Y amount from the other they've no idea what the cards are, or what the other cards within the pile contain. We'll then flip over the first card, which triggers the bidding stage. The three leaders taking part in this then place a number of Gems into their hand(s) equal to what they're willing to bid to get control of the item/unit. All players reveal their bids at the same time and the winner is the player with the most Gems; in cases of a tie then the tied players bid again, adding new Gems into their pots for the reward. If all players bid zero at any point then the reward is lost and no one picks it up, and it just makes it way back into the available rewards for next round. It's a very simple system, which has always been great fun and rewards players who are prepared to gamble on losing small rewards or and punishers those who overbid on expensive ones.

The Gems are of course also how you win the event, so captains need to work out if the single Talisman of Protection in the deck is worth spending those Gems on, or if they're better just letting everything pass, being at a small disadvantage in games but trying to win the overall event. The other part to consider here is that Gems spent here are actually spread between both the other teams and the "auctioneer" (for lack of a better term) with the "auctioneer" taking any odd number of Gems. As an example here a unit of Ogre Mercenaries is available and winning bid is a hefty eight Gems. As a result the winning bids faction loses eight Gems, two go to each of the other factions (meaning they've got some more to spend) but four go to the auctioneer who takes their cut and removes some wealth from the pool.

The idea is to create a fun mini game between rounds where players might really want certain items and their faction leaders have to try and balance things out knowing that there's a small chance those items may not be with their team for long or that the enemies might hold onto certain units for the entire campaign.

Mercenaries

I keep mentioning these units, and I think it's time to introduce you to some of them. These units grow as the size of the games increase over the weekend. As an example we've got a unit of Sellsword Crossbowmen, whose information is found below. As you can see it includes a full profile along with any rules for the unit. You'll see the unit size is split into 3 sections, 10/12/14; this represents the size of the unit depending on the size of the game they're involved in. At 750pts you'll get 10 of these fine young men, at 1500 they increase to 12 and at 2400 then you get the full 14.



We've got the following available for hire over the campaign weekend:

2x Sellsword Crossbowmen 2x Border Prince Bombards 1x Mercenary Outriders 2x Mercenary Ogres 1x Eshin Triad (character unit, Skaven Assassians) 1x Thorgrims Shields (character unit, Dwarf Warriors) 1x Alfglocks Defenders (character unit, Dwarf Crossbows) 1x Lucians Besiegers (character unit, Human Infantry)

The unit profiles for these are usually stolen directly from the Border Princes inspired Renegade Crowns army lists found in the War for Settra's Fury Arcane Journal or modified slightly from their army list entry in some way. Thorgrims Shields below are just a unit of Dwarf Warriors with a Thane imbedded in the unit with a couple of extra rules and a magic weapon on the character.



Once the event is over I'm going to take what I learn from the weekend and will publish all the entries once they've been updated (if needed) so everyone has access to these if you want to give it a go yourselves.

Magic Toys

I wanted to also have magic items be something of a commodity in the weekend and as a result we've got an added army list restriction where players can't purchase common magic items for their armies and can only take those linked directly to their force, with each character being limited to 1 such item. The idea here is that as the campaign progresses they'll find or purchase new items and dish them out to their characters. It also means that some of the items in the market phase, such as the Lore Familiar or Talisman of Protection will almost certainly fetch a really heavy price.

I haven't been particularly inventive here and have just copied all the common magic items from both the Main Rulebook and the Razing of Westerlands Arcane Journal. I've made a card for each of them for the table in the market phase as seen below and made a smaller copy of the card for players to place nearby the characters with the items to help them remember they've gotten additional loot during the games. I ended up using a Hearthstone Card Creator for this but would love to have a full set designed for it if my graphic design skills were up to more than they are (which they certainly are not).

Map Locations

The final campaign aspect I really wanted to bring into play over the weekend was to do with map control. As mentioned in the previous article we've got a map to fight over for the weekend players will be placing little dots in area's they've conquered or made their way to over the weekend. They'll be able to start taking over other teams realm is they win enough games conquering the no mans land between the factions and reach their opponents. In addition there are extra landmarks on the map such as the Wizards Towers, Merchant Camps and Mercenary Holdings.

Mercenary Holdings are pretty straight forward, while in control of one of those teams no longer have to pay the Upkeep on the Mercenary forces they have working for them in games, saving a pretty penny if their team captains have gone heavy into purchasing those. Merchant Camps grant a new Magic Item from the pile for the team for each game they hold it, meaning you've bypassing spending your Gems and getting a new item for the team, I expect these to be fought over.

The final type of landmark are the Wizard Towers, and I wanted to do something a little different with these. Wizard Towers tend to be gather points for likeminded wizards and as a result I wanted players to be able to lean on the powers within those towers when they capture them. This bring us to our final type of mercenary, the Tower Wizards. The thing is, however: Sometimes when you request help you get the brightest of the class, the grandmaster -- but other times you get wizards who are just on their first real outing since picking up the wand. These are represented by two special rules for the wizards; the first is that you don't really know if they're showing up or not, meaning teams with a wizard tower will roll off to see who gets said wizard. Second, you never know how good the wizard is, meaning you roll to get their Wizard Level as well. I've got a handful of Wizard models which should cover all styles and by having some on horseback and some on foot hopefully we'll have enough to go around and the teams will be able to pick the wizard to fit with their army requirements.

Character Progression

During campaigns characters develop and grow with their armies and trying to fit that into a weekend has been difficult. I've borrowed an idea from an old series of WHFB and AoS Events which used to run under the "Warhammer Achievement" umbrella which allowed hero's to "level up" similar to how Path to Glory can work. This should hopefully represent the chosen hero of the player growing from commanding this small 750pt force to grabbing their final mount and leading a 2400pt horde into battle. After each battle the player will get a benefit for their hero, this will leave some being slightly toned down from their normal "final form" iterations from the main books but I feel it's fairer this way, especially as players aren't paying any points for this hero. The idea here is that the reward you hero gains is always slightly better when you lose a game, after all you learn more from your mistakes right? I've got one of these for both the fighting and wizardly variety but below you can see the combat hero version.



I hope we to hear some exciting stories from games about how these characters progressed and what deeds they've accomplished over the weekend and I might even share some of the best ones in the final article for post event.

Rewards and Prizes

When it comes to this event I've tried to make it clear that the winning isn't the most important part and as a result the trophies for this event (of which there are 9, which isn't bad for 18 players) are designed to try and reward people from all area's of the Hobby. We've got prizes for;
  • Most Gems Earned by a player from each Faction (Order, Chaos, Destruction)
  • Most Bloodthirsty Player (player with the most VP's Overall, can't be won by someone who won the above)
  • Most Sporting Opponent
  • Favourite Army Voting (1st, 2nd and 3rd place)
  • and most importantly, in my opinion, the Best Set of Hero of Glory models.
It's always important to me that the goal of any weekend isn't just to "win" but to enjoy the games and embrace the overall feel of it and I hope that this spread of prizes manages to do that. I'm looking forward for judging the Hero of Glory models and I'm letting players vote for their favourite army, meaning they'll all get a say in who takes home those awards.

Final Thoughts

Anyway, that's all for now. As I'm writing this I've got a few trophies to finish off and the final few merc units to finish for the weekend so I'm going to get back to that and I'll see you all on the other side of the event.
