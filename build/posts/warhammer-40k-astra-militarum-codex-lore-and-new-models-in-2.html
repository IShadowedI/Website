Warhammer 40k Astra Militarum codex, lore, and new models in 2026

April 13th, 2026 via Wargamer

The Warhammer 40k Astra Militarum army - a.k.a. the Imperial Guard - is among the oldest and most popular playable factions in Games Workshop's sci-fi wargame. An enormous force of trillions of regular humans, the Guard are humanity's first - and often only - line of defense. This complete guide includes full details of the most recent Astra Militarum codex including the killer Death Korps of Krieg models - so read on for everything you need to collect and play Imperial Guard in 10th Edition!

For an explanation of all the other armies fighting it out in GW's grimdark galaxy, read our full guide to the Warhammer 40k factions. And to find out which armies are getting new rules next, check our updated list of Warhammer 40k codex release dates.

Use the links below to jump to what you need - from the latest new models, to the lore and background of the Astra Militarum and the various Imperial Guard regiments, to our breakdown of the 10th edition codex (which remains in play at the launch of Warhammer 40k 11th Edition, remember). I've even added glamor shots of my own Astra Militarum regiment - the 728th Nassau Hoplites - to show that even averagely painted Guard models can look badass!

New Astra Militarum models in 2026

After a year or so out of the spotlight, Imperial Guard fans are now getting a bevy of gifts, because Games Workshop is releasing five brand new Astra Militarum units in 2026, as part of 10th Edition's last hurrah: the Return of Yarrick narrative expansion.

  • Commissar Yarrick
  • Commissar Graves (on foot)
  • Commissar Graves (on Vigilance)
  • Centaur RSV
  • Hippogriff AFV

First off, we've got old Bale-Eye himself, Commissar Yarrick, back from the dead to lead Guard forces on Armageddon once again. But he's not the only unexpected Officio Prefectus reinforcements joining the war.

Commissar Graves (on foot)

Fearsome female commissar Thenia Graves is a new named character joining 40k in the Return of Yarrick storyline. We don't know much about her, except she can dual wield power fist and power sword (noyce) and, per WarCom, she's so obsessed with "ironclad discipline" she makes Yarrick look like the Good Cop. Yikes.

Commissar Graves (on Vigilance)

There's no doubting Commissar Graves is the new Billy Big Boots in Guard Town - because she comes with her own customised version of the new Centaur transport, named Vigilance. It's tripped out with everything an ostentatious Militarum leader needs: upgraded guns, lots of gold aquilas, a big regimental flag, and a heavy armored ram - because hanging out in the backlines is for cowards and heretics.

Centaur RSV

No, that's not a Bolt Action model; we're still in the 41st (or early 42nd) millennium, not World War Two. For reasons no doubt deliberately devised to have real world war historians grinding their teeth, the Guard are using half-tracks now - and we're 100% down with it. The Centaur RSV (Rapid Strike Vehicle) is a lighter, presumably faster alternative to the Chimera transport tank.

Hippogriff AFV

This isn't a WW2 miniature either, honest! The Hippogriff AFV (Armored Fighting Vehicle) is the Astra Militarum's first tabletop armored car, and appears to be a cheaper way to add mobile firepower to a mainly infantry based force.

With four primary weapon options (battle cannon, lascannon, onslaught gatling cannon, or melta cannon) and a choice of heavy stubber or meltagun as secondary, it's going to be a flexible little beast, too.

How on earth can you mount that big a cannon on a titchy little car? How the hell is it loading or ejecting shells? Don't ask questions. The Emperor Protects.

Who are the Astra Militarum?

In Warhammer 40k, the Astra Militarum (affectionately called 'The Guard') is the primary standing army of the Imperium of Man. It's composed of billions (possibly trillions) of unaugmented, ordinary human soldiers, drawn from planets all over the galaxy, and organised into millions of vast regiments.

Those countless human infantry soldiers are backed up by enormous numbers of tanks, support vehicles, and aircraft maintained by the Adeptus Mechanicus; supported by battlefield specialists including sanctioned psykers; and ferried to wars across the galaxy in the void-ships of the Imperial Navy.

The 'Guard' has existed, in various different structures and under different names, for over 10,000 years, since its original formation by the Emperor of Mankind in the late 30th Millennium to help fight his Great Crusade.

At that time it was called the Imperial Army (Imperialis Auxilia) - and would play a massive role in the many battles of the Horus Heresy civil war, with army regiments fighting alongside Space Marine legions on both the traitor and loyalist sides.

Of all the 40k armies to collect, play, and read books about, the Astra Militarum may be the most dearly beloved by fans. There are lots of reasons for that, but we reckon relatability is top of the list - these troopers are just regular old humans like you or I, and that means we can feel their pain, their fears, and their triumphs more keenly.

In the far future of the 41st millennium, the Emperor's domain is beset by dangers beyond comprehension, from nefarious Chaos Space Marines wreaking revenge on the empire that exiled them, to the encroaching alien menace of the Tyranid hive fleets - insectoid swarms bent on consuming the whole galaxy.

The Guard face all those terrifying foes with nothing but flimsy armor, low-power laser guns, old fashioned bayonets, and slow, clunky tanks slammed together on a budget - and they often win. That's a compelling theme for sci-fi wargamers.

Sure, the most famous defenders of humanity are the Space Marines, gene-crafted demigods who stand as a bulwark against the worst the universe has to throw at them. They're peerless in battle, but they're few, and can't win galactic wars on their own.

In their own way, the human soldiers of the Imperial Guard boast far more bravery, grit, and determination than even the Adeptus Astartes can muster.

Reasons to play Astra Militarum

The Astra Militarum are one of the 'classic' 40k armies, and there's no better time to collect and play them than now, with revamped models for not just the core Cadians, but specialist regiments and units like the Death Korps of Krieg, Rough Riders, and Ratling Snipers too.

But they're not without their drawbacks, and, like any 40k army, they won't suit everyone.

Play Astra Militarum if:

  • You enjoy a coordinated, multi-part strategy, and are comfortable with calculated losses.
  • You're more interested in shooting than melee assaults.
  • You like Big Guns And Tanks.

Don't play Astra Militarum if:

  • You aren't prepared to buy and paint a lot of models.
  • You aren't prepared to lose a lot of models every game.
  • You want to get into close combat - the Astra Militarum's very best melee units are really just there to counterpunch enemy assaulters, not to lead the charge.

Astra Militarum codex and rules for 11th edition

Games Workshop's latest Astra Militarum codex is the 10th Edition book, first released on its own on February 15, 2025. This remains valid into 11th Edition.

If you're new to 40k, this is the dedicated rulebook for the Astra Militarum army, which you'll need to play. Alongside some lore details, it contains your core army rule, all the datasheets which explain how your units, weapons, and abilities function in-game, and five distinct detachments you can build your army around.

Games Workshop has also released some additional free detachments which encourage different kinds of army building - you can find them in the downloadable faction pack on the Warhammer Community downloads page.

Army rule - Voice of Command

The Astra Militarum's core rule, Voice of Command, is all about using your army officers to yell Orders at the troops, giving the right bonuses to the right units, at the right times.

In the command phase at the beginning of each turn, each of your officers can issue one (or more) orders to a unit within six inches of them, and the target unit will get a specific benefit until the next command phase.

There are six orders available - here's what they do:

  1. Move! Move! Move! - Add 3 inches to target unit's move.
  2. Fix Bayonets! - Add 1 to target unit's Weapon Skill.
  3. Take Aim! - Add 1 to target unit's Ballistic Skill.
  4. First Rank, Fire! Second Rank, Fire! - Add 1 to Attacks for target unit's Rapid Fire weapons.
  5. Take Cover! - Improve target unit's save by one (max 3+).
  6. Duty and Honor! - Improve target unit's Leadership and Objective Control stats by 1.

Lower ranking officers can only issue one order per turn, but more powerful characters can hand out two or even three. Remember, though, that a unit can only be affected by one order at a time (unless some other ability lets them benefit from more).

Detachments

The 10th edition Imperial Guard codex has five detachments that -  unusually for an army with a reputation for only playing one way - genuinely offer distinctive ways to play and win games.

Each detachment has its own unique Detachment Rule which, together with the Army Rule, will define your core strategy by making you stronger in one particular area. Within each detachment, you also have a menu of four paid Enhancements to buff up your characters, and six custom Stratagems to activate for bonuses at crucial moments  during the game (at the cost of Command Points).

Here are the five detachments, with a breakdown of their key rule, and a summary of their best bits.

Combined Arms

Detachment Rule: Born Soldiers

  • Regiment units (most of your infantry) get the Lethal Hits ability when shooting non vehicle, non monster targets.
  • Squadron units (tanks and vehicles) get the Lethal Hits ability when shooting monster or vehicle targets.

Play Style

Balanced - Supports a mixture of infantry and vehicles, offense and defense.

Best Enhancement

Grand Strategist - given to an Officer model, allows them to issue one more order per turn

Best Stratagems

  • Reinforcements! (2CP) - After an Infantry Regiment unit dies, bring it back in Strategic Reserves at full strength
  • Flexible Command (2CP) - In the command phase, instead of the usual restriction to Regiment units, any number of officers can issue their orders to any units, including tanks.

Siege Regiment

Detachment Rule: Artillery Support

Each battle round, select one artillery barrage to fire:

  • Creeping Barrage - Roll a d6 for each enemy unit more than 12″ from your army. For every 5+, that unit is Shaken and takes -2 to move, advance, and charge rolls this battle round.
  • Incendiary Bombardment - Select 3 enemy units more than 12″ from your army. This battle round, those units are Scattered and cannot have the benefit of cover.
  • Smoke Shells - Select 3 friendly units. This battle round, those units have the Stealth ability (-1 to hit when shooting them).

Note: the second two options change to target only 2 units in smaller Incursion games, and 4 units in big Onslaught games.

Play style:

Massed infantry charge supported by artillery - the Death Korps of Krieg approach.

Best Enhancement:

Stalwart's Honors - given to an officer leading a unit, that unit gains the benefit of Take Cover! in addition to the benefit of any other order it's given.

Best Stratagems:

  • Callous Sacrifice (1CP)- Pick a Platoon unit in melee combat, and you can ignore the restriction on shooting the enemy units it's in combat with. But, every time you wound one of those enemy units this phase, roll 1d6 and your nominated unit loses a model for every 4+.
  • Over the Top (2CP) - one officer can issue the Move! Move! Move! order once to every eligible Infantry unit, regardless of range.

Mechanized Assault

Detachment Rule: Armored Fist

Add one to wound rolls when a unit shoots after disembarking from a transport.

Play style:

Aggressive, mobile infantry maneuvers using transports for board and objective control

Best Enhancement:

Bold Leadership - gives the bearer's unit (or any transport it's embarked in) 'sticky objectives' - objectives they capture remain in your control after you leave them, until the enemy takes them.

Best Stratagems:

  • Clear and Secure (1CP) - one infantry unit can re-roll all hits and wounds this shooting phase, providing it just disembarked from a transport.
  • Rapid Dispersal (1CP) - one infantry unit that just disembarked from a transport can immediately make a 6″ move.

Hammer of the Emperor

Detachment Rule: Iron Tread

All Squadron units (vehicles) automatically advance six inches instead of rolling, and can move through enemy units' engagement ranges (but not end moves there).

Play style:

TANKS!

Best Enhancement:

Calm Under Fire - Once per turn, when a vehicle officer (Tank Commander) issues an order to a Squadron unit, it can immediately issue the same order to another Squadron unit.

Best Stratagems:

  • Blazing Advance (1CP) - used on a Squadron unit that just advanced - it can still shoot despite advancing.
  • Furious Cannonade (1CP) - used on a Squadron unit in the shooting phase - Improve your ranged attack's AP by one if the target is within 12″.

Recon Element

Detachment Rule: Masters of Camouflage

Your Walker and Regiment units permanently have the benefit of cover - and when actually in cover, improve their save by another one (max 3+).

Play style:

High skill positioning gameplay, precision attacks, and deployment shenanigans to unbalance opponents.

Best Enhancement:

Tripwires - Each time an enemy unit ends a normal, advance, charge, or fall back move within 9″ of the bearer's unit, roll a d6. On a 4+ the enemy unit is stunned and gets -1 to all hit rolls until next turn.

Best Stratagems:

  • Draw them out (1CP) - One Platoon unit can make an immediate, reactive 6″ move when an enemy unit ends its normal, advance, or fall back move within 9″ of it.
  • Tanglefoot Grenades (1CP) - Used at the start of your opponent's charge phase on one friendly Grenades unit - until end of phase, all enemy charges against this unit take -2 to their charge roll.

As with all 10th edition detachments, each of these gameplay-distinctive ways to play the army is 'subfaction agnostic' - so it can be applied to any army list, regardless of which lore-appropriate (or home-brewed) regiment you want your Astra Militarum force to come from.

This is a departure from previous editions, where different rule sets within each faction were directly tied to officially mandated sub-factions like the Cadians, Catachans, or Armageddon Steel Legion. Some regiments from the lore do have specific units that lean more towards one detachment's strategy than others.

For example, the newly reinforced Death Korps of Krieg's Artillery Team has access to more powerful gunnery than the Cadians' Field Ordnance Battery, so might make for better fire support in a Siege Regiment army; that detachment is directly themed around GW's background fiction for the Krieg armies.

But it's all just down to personal preferences of theme and models. There are no formal restrictions on mixing Death Korps of Krieg, Cadian, and other models in your army, regardless of detachment choices - I'm thoroughly looking forward to welcoming Krieger reinforcements into my majority-Cadian force. 

Astra Militarum Combat Patrol

One of the prime benefits of an Astra Militarum force is the breadth of troops, models, and Imperial Guard tanks available to you. Whilst it's incredibly exciting to look at the variety you can choose from, it can also be a little overwhelming. Luckily, there's a definitive Warhammer 40k starter set for the army: the Astra Militarum Combat Patrol box.

Games Workshop brought out the most recent Combat Patrol box for the Guard in February 2025, featuring a frankly weird set of units, including some of its newer kits. It costs $170 / £100 direct from GW, but as usual you can find it for less at local game stores and via Amazon ($140 / £95).

The current Astra Militarum Combat Patrol box contains 25 miniatures, made up of these units:

  • Cadian Command Squad x 1 (five models)
  • Rough Rider x 10
  • Kasrkin x 10

As a force on its own, it plays an aggressive, high risk game. You'll need to lay down effective long range fire with your elite Kasrkin squad, supported with orders and buffs from the Command Squad - while you send the Rough Rider cavalry thundering up the board to take objectives and head off threats.

Played right, these units can kick out good damage every turn, with options for anti-tank weapons - but those vulnerable riders will die easily once they're in the thick of it, and you'll be lacking any cannon fodder or armored threats to divert enemy fire.

The box also lacks any reliable longer range, or indirect fire, guns to rain hell on the opposing force - a key Astra Militarum strength - and only has one Officer character to hand out orders, drastically limiting the effectiveness of your core army rule.

So: canny upgrades to the Combat Patrol army might be:

  • A Leman Russ battle tank - a cheap, tough, and fairly powerful gun platform to bully enemy lines and take heat off your Rough Riders.
  • A Field Ordnance Battery or Krieg Artillery Team - to add long range supporting firepower your opponent will have to deal with.
  • A second Officer, like a Cadian Castellan  - to double your orders.
  • Two squads of Cadian Shock Troops - basic infantry squads to push onto empty objectives and soak up fire if needed, keeping your more powerful Kasrkin alive longer to rack up kills.

Astra Militarum regiments

Throughout the Imperium, thousands of planets are compelled to form their own Imperial Guard regiments, conscripting many millions of troops in the name of the God Emperor. The Imperium is colossal beyond all imagination - so you can create your own Imperial Guard regiment, customising them to use the rules and paint schemes you prefer.

However, the setting features several 'official' Imperial Guard regiments that have become legends throughout the Imperium, and, as a result, have often had specific rules and histories in the game. Here are some of the most popular and famous regiments known to humanity - we'll start with the ruthless, gas-masked men and women of the hour…

Death Korps of Krieg

Of the many regiments, the intimidating Death Korps of Krieg are the one you'll see most of right now, as Games Workshop has just released a whole army set for them, with more brand new kits to come.

But just who are these grim-looking, trench-coated guys? In short, they're the most fatalistic, dogmatically loyal, and fearless warriors in the whole damn Guard - and that's because of their horrific, tragic backstory.

The planet of Krieg once suffered a civil war, with many trying to secede from the Imperium. After centuries of brutal trench warfare and atomic bombardment, the loyalists raised the Imperial aquila above a ruined world.

Today, in shame, the Death Korps of Krieg attempt to make up for this betrayal by fighting harder, suffering more, and pushing themselves past fear in service of the Imperium. Though the Krieg would never reject their duty, no matter how onerous, they have an unfounded reputation for seeking death. They know that their life belongs to the Emperor, and it is his to take when he wills it, not theirs to throw away.

Armageddon Steel Legion

The hive world of Armageddon is known for fending off a colossal horde of Orks on several separate occasions. As a result of the many invasions it's survived, the planet maintains a large Imperial Guard regiment, known as the Armageddon Steel Legion. They're a motorised infantry force, fielding many infantry squads in Chimaera transports, necessary for crossing the polluted wastes that cover much of Armageddon.

Cadian Shock Troopers

For millennia, the planet Cadia held vigil over the Eye of Terror, a hideous rent in space-time where reality overlapped with the raw stuff of Chaos. This proximity to humanity's most deadly enemies led the Cadian Shock Troopers to become one of the most famous and most numerous of all Astra Militarum regiments.

Alas, during Abaddon the Despoiler's 13th Black Crusade, Cadia was finally destroyed. The Cadians may have lost their home, but they have not lost their determination. As their last commander, Ursarkar E. Creed said, "The planet broke before the guard did". With untold millions of Cadians serving in wars across the galaxy, they fight on, taking the fight to Chaos wherever they can.

Catachan Jungle Fighters

This death world is a jungle paradise, until you set foot on it. Everything wants to kill you there, and we do mean absolutely everything. The Catachan Jungle Fighters make up the core of this Imperial Guard regiment, deadly foes who look down on anyone who hasn't suffered as they have.

The Catachan's have a reputation for strength and brutality, and have earnt the nickname "little Ogryns" - but don't let them hear you say that. They don't take to Imperial discipline too kindly, and there is a particularly high rate of mysterious accidents among Commissars assigned to Catachan regiments.

Mordian Iron Guard

The Mordian Iron Guard Regiment hails from the tidally locked hive world of Mordian. One half of the planet is a scorching desert pointed constantly towards the system star, while all human settlement exists in hives on the frigid dark side. A sinister world, its regiments have a reputation for ruthless discipline and efficiency.

Mordian has resisted repeated Chaos incursions, and has a proud history of dispatching regiments to fight in the Emperor's wars throughout the galaxy. The planet itself is located in Segmentum Obscurus on the far side of the Great Rift, meaning it is isolated in Imperium Nihilus. While all Mordian soldiers expect to die in duty, they now face the disquieting uncertainty that their home world is inaccessible and, possibly, lost.

Tallarn Desert Raiders

During the dark time of the Horus Heresy the world Tallarn was ravaged by an unforeseen invasion of the Iron Warriors. They rendered the planet toxic to all life with a virus bombardment, before commencing a ground assault in armored vehicles in search of some inscrutable prize.

The tank war that followed was the largest in the history of the Imperium, and its aftermath shaped the face of Tallarn for millennia since. Talarn is a lifeless desert world beset by dust storms whose population live in underground bunkers. The Tallarn Desert Raiders are experts in both light infantry combat and armored warfare, often using nimble infantry forces to harass, ambush, reconnoitre, and undermine the enemy before committing a decisive armored assault to finish the foe.

Valhallan Ice Warriors

Valhalla was a verdant world when it was first settled by humankind, and quickly became a bastion of civilization, but a collision with an iron meteorite drove it out of its orbit, rendering it into a frigid land of ice and death. If this environmental catastrophe were not enough, it was followed immediately by an invasion from an Ork warfleet.

The people of Valhalla proved their resilience against two terrible foes, starvation and the greenskin horde. The Valhallan Ice Warriors are winter warriors without compare, adept at surviving privation like few other regiments.

Vostroyan Firstborn

An industrial world, Vostroya has close links to the Mechanicus and Mars in particular. Their weapons tend to be well-maintained, out-performing other Imperial Guard regiments under similar circumstances. Their guard regiment, the Vostroyan Firstborn, may seem proud and ostentatious, but it is also a cover for a deep cultural shame.

During the Horus Heresy Vostroya was a hub of materiel manufacture for the Imperium. When the call to to defend the throne world against Warmaster Horus was issued, Vostroya refused to raise regiments, claiming that its manpower would be better served producing weapons for the war effort. Ever since it has pledged the firstborn children of its noble households to His service, to make amends for their laxity in the Imperium's direst hour.

Tanith First and Only

The plucky heroes of Dan Abnett's famous (and utterly fantastic) Gaunt's Ghosts novel series, the men and women Tanith First Light Infantry regiment have become living legends among the Imperial Guard.

The densely wooded planet Tanith raised three small regiments - mustering only 6,000 or so soldiers combined, plus a minimal complement of vehicles and artillery - but a catastrophic chaos assault hit the planet on the very day those regiments were formed, wiping out its entire population and nearly half the newly enlisted guardsmen.

Around 3,500 Guard survivors fled the planet's destruction, reforming into a single regiment - the morbidly named Tanith First and Only - under the leadership of Colonel Commissar Ibram Gaunt. Their thrilling exploits thereafter form the basis of Abnett's 16-book series Gaunt's Ghosts - named for the regiment's even more morbid nickname, which plays on both the Tanith's talent for sneaky warfare, and their tragic backstory.

The Tanith are masters of stealth reconnaissance, carrying forward skills learnt as hunters and trackers in the vast, dark forests which once covered their home planet. During the course of their many perilous and heroic postings in the Sabbat Worlds Crusade against Chaos, the Tanith First has picked up reinforcements from several other forces, most notably a complement of hardy citizens of the planet Verghast, who'd fought alongside them in the defense of Vervunhive during the events of the novel Necropolis.

Savlar Chem-Dogs

Not all Astra Militarum regiments are shining paragons of honor, duty, and loyal sacrifice to the throne. As a galaxy-wide totalitarian power, the Imperium has millions of prisons bursting with scum and villainy. So, naturally, like the Soviet Red Army that inspired so much of its lore, the Guard has penal legions: massive regiments of conscripted convicts, dredged up from the very nastiest corners of an already horrible society.

Foremost among those weaponized dregs of humanity are the infamous Savlar Chem-Dogs. Their home planet, Savlar, is a lawless, poisonous hell hole - used by the Imperium as a giant mine to harvest toxic chemicals, and a dumping ground for vast shipments of criminals and captives, all under the supervision of merciless Adeptus Arbites guards.

Savlar's unbreathable, chem-choked atmosphere means its people permanently wear their distinctive rebreather masks - and the horrendous living conditions imposed on everyone unfortunate enough to live there have turned its population into ruthless, self serving cut-throats, capable of unspeakable brutality (but only if there's something in it for them).

Only the Imperium could look at such a place and think 'we should make them fight' - but it found a way. Savlar offered its prisoners a ticket off their prison planet, in exchange for almost certain death fighting in the absolute worst battlefields imaginable - and, crucially, license to loot and salvage anything they might find there.

And so the Chem-Dogs were born: an army of vicious, amoral scavengers, treated as completely expendable by their commanders, and deployed explicitly to do as much damage as possible, then die.

Elysian Drop Troops

It's well known that every human army in science fiction needs its own painfully cool, far future version of paratroopers. In the Astra Militarum, that role is filled by the rarely seen, fan favorite Elysian Drop Troop regiments.

Lightly armored and equipped with back mounted grav-chutes and lightweight Accatran-pattern lasguns, Elysian Drop Troops specialize in quick, overwhelming surprise attacks from the air, or stealthy aerial insertions by Valkyrie dropship, followed by guerrilla warfare campaigns. If you get strong vibes of US Airborne divisions striking behind German lines on D-Day in 1944, that's because you're supposed to!

Even though their homeworld of Elysia is right in the middle of the Imperium, in Armageddon's backyard, we don't see much of the Drop Troops in 40k lore. That's partly because, due to their expensive, specialist equipment and inherent lack of armored support, they're only deployed in quite specific engagements, and rarely in vast numbers.

But - as proven by Halo ODST and Helldivers 2 - nerds love sci-fi paratroopers, making the Elysian Drop Troops a firm favorite. And by "nerds", we mean us. We love 'em.

How to convert Astra Militarum models

Out of all the armies in Warhammer 40k, the Imperial Guard are maybe the best positioned for conversions; hell, even an Airfix kit can be easily taken apart and used for an upgrade or visual change for your army.

The Astra Militarum allows for a huge degree of customization, with many stunning armies being made up of innovative and creative Imperial Guard conversions. As the entire design behind the Astra Militarum takes inspiration from World War II and World War I armies, you can easily mix in third-party parts or models from ranges like Warlord Games' Bolt Action, or even Black Powder, to spice up your army's visual appeal.

At the very easiest level, you can very easily personalize tanks by modifying infantry models to ride along on their hulls - or cut the legs off officers and stick them in the cupola to become Tank Commanders, as I've done with a Militarum Tempestus Tempestor Prime and a Leman Russ in the photo below.

Many companies, such as Pig Iron Productions, Victoria Miniatures (whose Guard-flavoured 'Scrapper's Bag of Bits' product image you see above), or Anvil Industry also make troops that combine history with science fiction, making their models and conversion bits ideal for your Imperial Guard Conversions. Some companies offer a wider range of female model options, such as the excellent Bad Squiddo Games.

For plastic sci-fi regiments, you've got a few options. Wargames Atlantic produce several ranges - their own extremely varied Death Fields, and the Iron Core and SpaceNam kits made in partnership with other designers. Mantic Games have a great range of infantry and vehicles in the GCPS line. And then there's Warlord Games' Konflikt '47, which - while mostly metal and resin - has a growing plastic line focused on World War 2 superscience.
