Games Industry News Roundup- April 28th, 2026

April 28th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Here at Tabletop Battles, we know that it’s hard to keep track of all the news happening all the time in the games industry. So much is always going on, all over the place, all the time. With games of all sorts, and their related media, being an informed gamer can feel impossible some days. 

That's why every week, we help you stay in the know by rounding up five of the biggest recent stories in the gaming and gaming-related pop culture sphere in the Games Industry News Roundup. Our trusty news boy, Dan “Swiftblade” Richardson, is right here at the very real, cool, and newly refurbished TTB newsdesk with the scoop.

CMON Invests Millions in NFT Video Game Studio to “Stay Relevant”

Credit: CMON Games

CMON is a frequent flier here on the roundup, and each week one is left thinking, “This is it. This is the apex of the CMON dumpster fire. This cannot get any worse or any weirder.” Time and time again, CMON has made fools of us all.

This time, CMON has decided to hitch its future on the digital market, and announced that the company is investing $2.1 million in the blockchain-based games company, Blissful Link. 

According to a report released by CMON to the Hong Kong Stock Exchange (HKSE), CMON purchased a 2.2% stake in Blissful Link on April 23rd. With this purchase, CMON intends to work with Blissful Link as a partner in pushing CMON IP into the digital and Web3 realm, with Massive Darkness and Super Fantasy Brawl Reborn planned to be the first IPs adapted. 

“The company believes that in order to continue to be relevant in the games industry and to expand the group’s revenue stream, the group would need to conduct digital transitioning and venture into video game development and Web3 projects.” CMON writes in its statement to the HKSE. “Digital transitioning would have the benefits of enhanced visual effects, have apps that handle scoring, timing etc [sic], would enable a diversified number of players and are more accommodating to players not within the same vicinity.”

CMON elaborates further that despite pushing into digital games, it still intends to develop and offer physical tabletop games as a way to foster a “screen break” and encourage “direct face to face interaction”. 

CMON also believes that moving into the Web3 space will emphasize the company’s commitment to social responsibility, writing “Added to this, entering into Web3 projects often emphasise social responsibility and ethical practices such as transparency and fairness on decision marking. By participating in Web3 projects, the company can demonstrate its commitment to social responsibility and sustainability.” 

If the decision for CMON to purchase $2.1 million in shares in any company following its most recent annual report, which stated a $19.9 million loss in FY 2025, seems dubious at best, then the decision to invest in Blissful Link in particular is outright baffling. Blissful Link is a web3 video games company founded in 2021 by Hong Kong developers (though for tax purposes, the company is headquartered in the British Virgin Islands). Blissful Link is primarily responsible for the release of Capverse, a NFT game where players purchase avatars called “Sumers” to battle with online.

Blissful Link itself has been valued at $95 million by outside investors, but the financials for the company shared in CMONs report are dire. Blissful Link reported a loss of $197,000 in 2024 according to CMONs report, and the Capverse website has not been updated since 2024 either. CMON did not provide any of Blissful Link’s 2025 finances in the HKSE report.   

Gloomhaven Creator Steps Down and Cephalofair CEO

Credit: Cephalofair Games

Isaac Childres, the creator of board game megahit Gloomhaven, is stepping down from his role as CEO of Cephalofair Games to exclusively focus on games design. 

Despite stepping down from his CEO role, Childres will still be the owner of Cepalofair, with his new title becoming “Owner/Lead Games Designer”. Longtime COO Price Johnson will step up into the CEO role, having worked in the COO position for 9 years and served as a close collaborator with Childres during that time. 

“Working with Isaac since the early release days of Gloomhaven to grow both Cephalofair and our community has been an absolute privilege and highlight of my career.” Price wrote in a statement. “We have come a long way since the early days of indie game design and crowdfunding, while learning a lot about ourselves and this industry in the process. I can't wait to share the exciting plans we have creatively and organizationally in this next chapter with our fans, but that starts by putting in the hard work alongside our amazing team, whom I thank for their trust and support.”

Julie Ahern, the former COO and Vice President of Greenbriar Games, is replacing Price as the COO of Cephalorfair Games. Ahern brings with her 15 years of industry management experience, having worked on releases like Folklore: The Affliction and Zpocalypse with Greenbriar games as well as titles such as Final Girl and Tales of Myth and LEgend during her most recent tenure as Senior Director of Operations with Van Ryder Games.

“Julie is my absolute favorite person in the board game industry (sorry Price!), and I could not be happier to welcome her to the team,” Childres writes. “I think she is the perfect fit for a needed role in this transition that allows me to focus on my core passion: designing epic, strategic games. Thanks Julie!"

Modiphus Delays Release Schedule Due to Global Shipping Issues

Credit: Bethesda Games Studios/Modiphus Entertainment

Modiphus Entertainment has become victim to the global shipping catastrophe caused by the ongoing conflict between the United States and Iran. According to the April Fallout Friday post on Modiphus’s website, supply chain issues are forcing the upcoming release schedule and preorders back until later this summer.

Further details on what exactly is being delayed and by how much is provided in the post itself. The Modiphus team states that the Institute and Super Mutant faction core boxes are being bumped back to the end of July, alongside the Battle for Boston set which was delayed earlier this month. The Minutemen and Railroad boxes are getting pushed back a few weeks more, and are now expected to drop in the middle of August.

Alongside these faction delays, Modiphus is pushing back the preorder window for its upcoming new edition of Fallout: Wasteland Warfare. Instead of opening preorders in May, Modiphus will instead start preorders sometime in Q3, with the books set to ship sometime in Q4.

“We really appreciate your understanding. No one is more disappointed in this situation than we are, and we are committed to getting these games to you as soon as possible.” Modiphus writes. “We can’t wait to show you all the hard work that’s been poured into the new edition, but as it stands, we’ve still got a little way to go.”  

Magic: The Gathering Arena Team Unionizes

Credit: United Wizards of the Coast

Hell must’ve frozen over this week, because both of the stories related to video games we’ve got on the slate this week are shockingly positive.

The development team for Magic: The Gathering’s digital analogue, Arena, announced that their development team has passed a supermajority vote to unionize under the CWA, becoming the United Wizards of the Coast (UWOTC) union.

“This is a massive moment for the Arena team, and we’re thrilled to embark on this journey with you all.” the United Wizards of the Coast union writes. “Our demands aim to preserve our rights and wellbeing as workers. This is a step forward not just for the Arena team, but the gaming industry as a whole. We’re demonstrating that a phenomenal team of game makers can come together to create something truly magical.”

Following the successful vote, the union is now engaged in trying to bring Wizards and its parent company, Hasbro, to the bargaining table. In a letter sent to Wizards and Hasbro leadership, the United Wizards of the Coast team has laid out multiple demands from corporate leadership. These points are focused on protecting the workers at UWOTC as well as improving their working environment, and include items such as protections against generative AI, clear career paths, ownership by workers for anything created in their free time, guaranteed remote work opportunities, and defense against layoffs. 

“We call on Hasbro and Wizards of the Coast to voluntarily recognize our union and to publicly commit to remaining neutral during our unionization efforts—not just toward the MTG Arena team, but to all Hasbro employees and unionization efforts, present and future.” UWOTC writes. “We believe in building bridges with our peers on other teams, not divisions. Respecting neutrality ensures a fair and democratic process for everyone.”

The UWOTC team has filed a petition with the National Labor Relations Board to register the union, though UWOTC team has offered to withdraw said petition should the leadership at Wizards and Hasbro recognize their union voluntarily and come to the negotiating table prior to May 1st. As of this writing, neither Wizards or Hasbro has publicly commented on the union.

Microsoft Announces Game Pass Price Cut

Credit: Xbox Game Studios/Microsoft

Microsoft is walking back some of their Game Pass price hikes made earlier this year, with the Xbox team announcing that the pricing plans for two of their Game Pass packages will be lowering the monthly subscription cost effective immediately.

In October 2025, Microsoft drastically increased the price of all of its Game Pass models by around 50%, creating public backlash against a service that was already dropping in popularity with the gaming community. The April 2026 price cuts to the Ultimate and PC Game Pass services don’t fully undo these increases, but are a significant step back in price, with the monthly fee for Ultimate Game Pass going from $29.99 to $22.99 and the PC Game pass dropping from $16.49 to $13.99.

The newly discounted price tag does come with a catch, as new Call of Duty titles won’t be available on launch for Games Pass. Rather, new Call of Duty titles will come to the platform in the following holiday season after release. Since the franchise has for many years operated on an annual release schedule, this change is likely to ensure strong sales for each yearly title to make up for the revenue lost from Games Pass.

“Our players cover a wide breadth of geographies, preferences, and tastes, so while there isn’t a single model that’s best for everyone, this change responds to a lot of feedback we’ve gotten so far. We’ll continue to listen and learn,” the Xbox Team writes. “Thank you for being a part of the Xbox community.”
