Tabletop Battles Reviews: Armageddon The Return of Yarrick - Armoured Gauntlet

April 25th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Monster and Vehicle enjoyers rejoice, for at long last you have a beautiful set of additional rules built around encouraging both players to bring their collection of METAL BAWKES and Stompy Bitey critters for a quick three game campaign that could be run over one day for two people/teams.

We’d like to thank Games Workshop for providing us with an advance copy of these rules for review purposes.

Rainbow Warriors Impulsor by Craig "MasterSlowPoke" Sniffen

With an awesome Damage mechanic that in all honesty should have been included with the recent Apocalypse release and a bevy of powerful upgrades, these rules could be very easily slapped into a Crusade Campaign or run on their lonesome.

Throughout this expansion and review, the word Spearhead will pop up quite a bit as the rules in this book are meant for the bigger Monsters and Vehicles. Spearhead is a keyword that is added to any Monster or Vehicle with 10 wounds or more with exceptions for Aircraft and Fortifications, who will miss out… Suck it Space Elves and Hammerfall Bunker owners.

Photo Credit: Musterkrux

Beanith: So unfair, my beloved Metal dreadnought missed out by a whisker. Ah well, an excuse to build those other Primaris dreadies in my collection I suppose.

Spearhead units handle damage a little differently than the rest of the units on the table. Those familiar with 8th Edition Apocalypse may remember Damage Tokens and may even have some laying around with the movement trays which will come in handy. Whenever an attack from a unit that would take a Spearhead model to zero wounds, instead of removing the model, you would roll a D6 on a specific Damage Table and add +1 to the result for any existing Damage Tokens it might have. Any further attacks or damage from that attacking unit is discarded.

Onager Dunecrawler - Icarus Array. Credit: Rockfish

Beanith: Making sure you properly split-fire will be key in this expansion no matter how funny it would be to see the look on your mate’s face when told seven of the nine Firepike shots were wasted

There are three basic tables covering Transports, Monsters and Vehicles, as well as each Faction having their own versions to mix things up. Assuming you don’t end up with a result of 6+, the Spearhead model will end up with some Damage Tokens and Damage Status depending on what was rolled. Damage Status mostly reduces various characteristics but luckily for the target don’t stack if you roll the same result again on the table.

Armoured Gauntlet Basic Damage Table - Credit Warhammer Community

Once you’ve forced a 6+ on any of the Damage Tables, the Spearhead unit is finally destroyed and we finally get to Beanith’s favorite part: If the model has the Deadly Demise ability, when rolling to see if you get an earth shattering kaboom, you add +1 to the result for each Damage Token that was on that unit.

Spearhead Upgrades

It's not all about the size of the crater and how many models you manage to take with you when you eventually blow up, there are also a series of Spearhead Upgrades available with each Faction getting three to choose from. Each can only be taken only once and can’t be stacked on the same Spearhead model.

War Dog Stalker. Credit: Rockfish

The number of Spearhead Requisition Points available depends on the combined points value of Spearhead units in your army. In a standard 2000 point game, those that leave the good stuff at home (<500) get nothing and those that go overboard (>1500) will have SRP to burn.

Beanith: The Designers recommend both sides have roughly the same number of points spent on Spearhead units to help balance out the shenanigans. That said, there are Challenge Missions designed for those playing with less Spearhead models than their foe.

Beanith: With 70+ Spearhead upgrades, I am just going to cherry pick some of my favorites.
  • Imperial Guard (I will sulk if an editor corrects this to the Astra whosits) can spend 3SRP for Auto-Repair Shrine which removes Damage tokens when this unit passes Battle-shock tests.
  • Grey Knights can slap Psychic Might on their Psyker vehicles adding +2 STR to Psychic weapons for 2SRP.
  • Imperial Knights will always have the Ion Capacitor Shrine for 3SRP because who doesn’t love seeing a 4+ Invun save against ranged attacks on a Knight Castellan?
Imperial Knight Castellan. Credit: Jack Hunter
  • Speaking of tabletop crimes, the fishie bishie T'au have managed supe up the Advanced Repair Systems, rolling a D6 for each Damage Token it has and removing them on a 4+

Armoured Gauntlet Missions

This expansion contains 12 missions, the first six missions have the usual symmetrical deployment zones and objectives for both sides to scrap over. There is no terrain layout beyond where to place objectives so that's entirely left to the players. You may want to focus leaving enough room for the big vehicles to manoeuvre but not too much as to create planet bowling ball and turn it into a shooting gallery.

TX7 Hammerhead Gunship. Credit: Rockfish

Each mission has one or two unique Mission rules which tend to tie into the Mission Objective scoring. The Heavy Metal mission has Escalating Battle which lets your Spearhead units turn an objective marker ‘sticky’ so they can wander off next turn if needed. The other is Kill Tally where you keep track of enemy units destroyed (Spearhead units are worth 3 unless its Titanic which case its 5, and everyone else is 1) which ties to the Wreck and Ruin Objective which rewards 5VP for gaining at least 1 Kill Tally and an extra 5VP for the player with the higher tally that round.

The next three missions are asymmetric objectives along with the less traditional deployment zones. Armoured Breakthrough wants the Attacker to drive off the other side of the battlefield and maybe push the Defender off objectives along the way. Oddly enough, the Defender would rather the Attacker stick around and exit in a more explosive fashion.

Genestealer Cult trucks. Credit: Michael O "mugginns"

The remaining three are Gauntlet Challenge Missions designed for those playing with less Spearhead models than their foe with the example given as less than 25% of their army are Spearhead units. That person will be the Defender in all of the Gauntlet Challenge missions and the cool dude with three Baneblades is clearly the Aggressor/Attacker in these scenarios. All three missions are asymmetric objectives along with the wonky deployments. The Steel Gauntlet mission wants the Attacker to push deep into the Defenders deployment zone with more VP rewarded the further you go and the Defender would rather you’d sod off.

Armoured Gauntlet Campaigns

And as expected for these End of Edition expansion books, the Warhammer team has included a set of rules to run a short three game campaign where your Spearhead units can rack up kills and gain cool upgrades to help rack up more kills in the final battle.

Armoured Gauntlet Campaign - Credit Warhammer Community

There are three campaign trees branching out in various ways depending on who won and rewards the victor with bonuses. We tend to like these as they do offer a fair amount of replayability and in theory you could power through this campaign in a day.

Ace Upgrades

During the campaign, each Spearhead unit keeps track of the number of other spearhead units they destroy, which will unlock an upgrade for the rest of the campaign. Each Upgrade is unlocked at a Spearhead unit’s 1st kill, 3rd kill and 5th kill.

Squig Destroyer, Goff Orks Battlewagon. Credit: Charlie Brassley

The first upgrade is random (Beanith: The proper way), and the second and third upgrades can then be chosen. Keep in mind you can’t have the same one more than once per model so no stacking Ablative Plating reducing Armour Penetration so you can shrug off Rail Rifles/Laser Destroyers. Other upgrades include Juggernaut which lets you yeet yourself through terrain features at the risk of becoming Battle-shocked, and Tank Hunters get to reroll 1s to hit and wound other Monster and Vehicles. They also are better at spotting the flashing red weak points as well meaning you can add 1 to the Damage table results.

Rounding out the campaign section is Field Repairs where before the second and third battle, Spearhead units that were destroyed must roll a D6 on the Field Repair table. Rolling a 2 or 3 means they start the battle with a Damage Token or worse, rolling a 1 makes them roll on the appropriate Damage Table and start the game with that effect. They were kind enough to let you reroll 6’s whilst still mean enough to also reroll 1’s on the Damage tables. If the dice gods are kind and the duct tape supplies plentiful, you will roll a 4+ and start the next battle Fully Restored.

Sector Mechanicus "Tanks". Credit: Charlie Brassley for the terrain and the terrible joke is Beanith's

Those running around with a Character unit that can heal vehicles (Techmarines, Mekboy, whoever) can reroll on the table, which is handy if you roll a 2 or 3 but pointless if you roll a 1 because it is written in the stars that a 1 will always reroll into another 1.

Final Thoughts

Beanith: This shit is amazing and criminal in how it's one of the last things dropped before the new edition. I would have loved seeing this earlier and it would have been amazing working alongside the Monsters And Those Who Hunt Them stuff from Tyrannic Wars.

It’s Apocalypse part two. All the big things can survive a little longer and best of all they are almost guaranteed to blow up when they eventually do die. This should have been crusade content and I hope this is still largely compatible with 11th Edition due out later this year.

Its interesting on how you get the Spearhead Upgrades and that the upgrades are unique and can't be stacked on a single model. There's three per faction and priced in such a way you can't take all three unless you lean right into tanks and monsters which is my jam.

But thats all just dressing, its the damage tables, damage tokens and better chance of deadly demise that has my attention. I'm going to fling so many Rhinos at people whilst blurbing on about the METAL BAWK meme.
