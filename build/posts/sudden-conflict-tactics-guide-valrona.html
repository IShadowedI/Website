Sudden Conflict – Tactics Guide: Valrona

April 24th, 2026 via Goonhammer
This article is part of a series of sponsored articles from Sudden Conflict.

Welcome once again, to another Sudden Conflict Tactics Guide. We’ve talked about Sudden Conflict before. It’s a cool 3v3 skirmish game that has just been released on Gamefound. Goonhammer has been lucky enough to receive review copies of each of the release boxes and after some tinkering and experimentation we’ve come up with some guides for how to build teams and play with the various characters in each set.

The set we’re looking at today is the realm of Valrona, a setting probably best described as late Middle Ages or early modern Europe. You've got the Ashen Plague, the ancient and mysterious Black Forest (the Black Forest Cake is a lie, you'll only find monsters here) and all of the palace intrigue you can handle, what with the Queen Anastasia struck down with some mysterious illness (perhaps even worse) and several players seeking to use this power vacuum to their own advantage. Even as it all burns down, there's always someone looking to score points over their rivals...

The Tryptic Vile

The Tryptic Vile - Sudden Conflict (photo courtesy of Musterkrux)

Gulmar

Gulmar - Sudden Conflict

Gulmar is what happens when you stick a bunch of animal corpses into a Horadric Cube and windmill slam that 'Transmute' button. Gulmar is also the noise you hear when you open up that cube and turn it upside down and shake out whatever's inside. In a world in which the Ashen Plague has reduced fertility to nil, Gulmar is the first 'birth' (and we use that term incredibly lightly) in a long time. I imagine there was a lot of confusion at the gender-reveal party when they sliced the cake and black ichor oozed out if it: "It's a...sin against nature?" (Note: Gulmar is identified as a 'he' in the rulebook, we're just having fun). Gulmar is a very solid all-rounder, he's got solid mobility (which includes a Momentum generation option), has an absolutely stonking good melee attack, and has a little bit of status manipulation: He can gain Bless tokens, inflict Hex, as well as sometimes gain Curse tokens (that's bad, though).

 

Strengths

That Unearthly Strength, yo. A 4-dice melee attack that also introduces a Push for extra damage is savage. Having the ability to move and generate Momentum without rolling dice (Determined Shamble) and only using a single action is a really efficient ability to have in your toolbox. From the Shadows has the potential to really surprise your opponent, as a 3-dice roll with x2 successes in movement can go a long distance when you're ignoring barriers.

 

Weaknesses

Worth noting that Tethered Soul is both a (wait for it) Blessing and a Curse. The last thing you want when you're down to your last active character is for someone like Gulmar to start stacking Curse tokens with no efficient way of stripping them. Your opponent might aim to bring down Gulmar last if you bring him into a match up, because he's really going to struggle to be effective if he's the last man/monstrocity standing.

Additionally, unless you're blowing momentum on the attack, I think Terrifying Wave is more of an incidental Momentum farmer (generally, when you roll dice and don't succeed, you gain a momentum) than a bona fide attack skill. Maybe use it to clean up a model with one wound remaining or when you're desperate to drop a Hex on a model that's about to activate and slap you silly? However, most of the time I don't rate this ability.

Sidenote: Tethered Soul names 'Krandus' as the focus of this ability, at least in the review copy we got, but assume it actually means Gulmar as the literal reading doesn't make sense.

 

Good Partners

Gulmar can't strip his own status effects easily and with most of his offensive power coming from a melee attack (but what an attack!) I reckon his best friend would be someone who can help enhance his movement, strip debuffs and potentially farm momentum for him. As such, good team-mates include: Baron Halloway (Galactic Throne, who just brings it all) and potentially even Belle Thrushwood (please ignore the fact they sit on opposite sides of the Valrona conflict).

Rating: A. Gulmar brings a lot to any team he joins and doesn't have any dealbreaker weaknesses, so he's an easy pick for many players.

Ginger

Ginger - Sudden Conflict

Ginger is a lovely person, someone who has an absolutely normal number of joints in her arms and her hands are a perfectly charming and normal size. Please do not use the word polydactyly around Ginger, because she has exactly the right number of fingers and you shouldn't question this. At least, not if you want to keep your trachea inside your thorax. Dark magic isn't cheap these days, dark magic costs hella organs and Ginger doesn't work for free.

 

Strengths

All of Ginger's abilities are really useful. Movement 5 and the ability to move through models makes it hard to pin her down. She's got three different ways to manipulate status effects, Blessing herself and allies, as well as tossing around Hexes and Curse tokens willy-nilly. And Consuming Malefice is a decent Ranged attack that gives you a free Action if you knock someone down.

 

Weaknesses

Ginger is very solid all around. Her biggest drawbacks would be the complete lack of directed Momentum-farming and the fact that all of her attacks max out at 3-dice before momentum, so she needs a bit of help from her team to land the really big hits (Her Bless tokens help but it never hurts to just simply roll more dice).

 

Good Partners

As above, Ginger wants Momentum to really turn it up to 11. So, we turn to the Usual Suspects of Belle Thrushwood and Baron Halloway but I'm also fond of Rynola (from Ukyo), who brings a little bit of Momentum game to the team, as well as a solid bit of biffo. I'm not sure what Ginger thinks of Rabbit organs as a currency for channeling dark magic, though.

Rating: B. Ginger is very solid but doesn't stand out in any single field.

Nikola Marcellus

Nihola - Sudden Conflict

If you told Nikola Marcellus that you knew what he is, he'd probably say 'Yeah, a vampire. Duh.' and then tear open your throat. So, umm, don't do that. Every ability Nikola has is vampire-coded and it's all pretty cool, honestly.

 

Strengths

Nikola has two great mobility options. Most models would kill to have Calyptra Swarm (4-dice, move Successes x2 while ignoring Barriers) but I feel like he really should be using Wolf in Sheep's Clothing to generate Momentum as often as possible (also, he really wants to be up in people's grills, so it's win-win, honestly)

Hypnotic Bite is a really good ability, all characters can deal damage, some can heal, only Nikola can do both as part of the same action. Anything you can do to change the delta/difference between your team's total HP count and your opponent's is a step towards victory and Nikola is just straight up double dipping here. A Healing mechanic is also amazing for a character who can just straight up resurrect himself with Eternal Damnation. Personally, I recommend playing incredibly ham with Nikola (this also feeds into his Momentum farming by ending moves adjacent to enemies). You want your opponent throwing their swings at the guy who will either heal the damage back up or go full Goldie Hawn and just stand back up after a quick nap. Even if your opponent tries to ignore Nikola and chase after your less, shall we say, immortal characters, you can use him as a body-blocker and run interference the whole game until your opponent gets sick of his shenanigans and tries to put him down, if only for a round or two.

 

Weaknesses

Nikola has a little bit but not a huge amount of Status Effect game, and his attacks are just OK at 3-dice native. This is all understandable, as his kit needs to be balanced around the fact that he's near-on immortal and hard to knock of the game entirely. Just make sure you save 1-2 Momentum dice for when he needs to roll for Eternal Damnation.

 

Good Partners

Nikola loves delivering Hypnotic Bite, so anything you can do to debuff any enemy or buff Nikola before he goes in for a snack is always welcome. Additionally, any healing you can provide to top him up after he drags himself back into the fight is very, very welcome. As such, Nikola is best friends with Ula. Heather Sing might also be an interesting, as she gives him a Glory token any time she activates and he's lying down on the job in line of sight. Otherwise, as antithetical as it is to admit, Belle Thrushwood probably does a good job keep Nikola buffed.

Rating: A+. Eternal Damnation is an amazing ability and the only reason why Nikola isn't S-tier is because the rest of his kit is balanced against this amazing ability.

Nikola asks his new 'friends' to deal with these colourful invaders to Valrona. - Sudden Conflict

The Ashen Alliance

The Ashen Alliance - Sudden Conflict (photo courtesy of Musterkrux)

Krandus Moore

Krandus - Sudden Conflict

Growing up in an orphanage with a name like Krandus is, perhaps, the most grueling forge in which you could temper the steel within a man. Children can be so cruel. Krandus is a very strong frontline fighter, tip of the spear and all that. He charges in, swings hard and then repositions to either set up your team or disrupt the enemy formation.

 

Strengths

Attacks with a native dice-value for 4 or higher in this game are rare enough to call out when you see them, so Nimble Assault gets a very positive evaluation from me. It's a 4-dice swing followed by a move afterwards, so this is an excellent ability. Resolute Charge means that you can launch Krandus up the table quite a distance, possibly scoop up a Bless token and then swing with Nimble Assault before either retreating or burrowing even deeper into the enemy lines.

Krandus has a little bit of Status Effect manipulation, he gains Bless tokens very easily and can sometimes Hex people but a 2-dice attack is rarely a sure thing.

Last Stand is cute, if you can pull it off you'll get an Action back (possibly more if you spend Momentum) and that'll give you enough juice to move (gaining a Bless token along the way, hopefully) and then Assaulting someone Nimbly before collapsing again.

 

Weaknesses

For a man that is absolutely swimming in Bless tokens (re-roll all non-successful dice and then discard all of your Bless tokens), Krandus doesn't have a lot of abilities that really leverage this well (Also, he can only give them to himself, not his team; very greedy). Nimble Assault is an obvious target for spending a Bless token but I'd be really worried about committing one of my few Actions per turn to a 2-dice Fist of Glory or a single die for Last Stand, flubbing the dice roll and then flubbing it again on unlucky dice. Perhaps you can look at it as an opportunity to give him some slightly more reliable outcomes for those actions but I'd worry that you're chasing good money after bad by committing your Bless tokens to that.

Krandus also doesn't have an explicit Momentum farming mechanic, so he's relying on his allies (or failed Fists of Glory*) to generate Momentum for himself and his team.

 

Good Partners

Krandus wants a bit of Momentum, so it's very convenient that his Commander in the Queen's Legion is Belle Thrushwood.

Rating: C. This might actually be the lowest ranking I've given a character in Sudden Conflict. Krandus is...fine. Nimble Assault is a load-bearing ability on his profile but after that I'm not sure I love what I'm seeing. Sorry, Krandus.

Belle Thrushwood

Belle - Sudden Conflict

Belle is a bit of an enigma; their background reads as very ambiguous as to whether they're an agent for good or evil in this setting. Certainly one for stability, though. Maybe in these uncertain times, that's exactly what the people of Valrona need? Belle is potentially the greatest status effect manipulator in the game; most of their abilities either add or strip buffs and debuffs from both allies and enemies.

 

Strengths

Belle is a Momentum farming machine, just by moving you can easily get 2 Momentum each time they activate thanks to Survey the Troops.

Thunder Rifle is rad, a 3-Dice ranged attack, with a Push that'll get you another point of damage if you're lucky, is very strong.

Another big draw for Belle is their Unconventional Strategist and Rallying Speech, just toss those buffs around and take away anything you don't want to see. Rallying Speech only needs to land a single success to hook up both of your buddies with a Bless token each.

 

Weaknesses

I'm not sure Belle has any significant weaknesses. Maybe their lack of kooky mobility skills? The Bless token awarded at the end of Duelling Sabre might be a little unconventional in timing (you'd rather have the Bless token to make the attack but I suppose it'll help for you next Defence roll?) but that's not really a weakness.

 

Good Partners

Belle is a force-multiplying support piece. They're not looking for a good partner so much as it's other characters asking Belle to dance. Basically, anyone who has great offensive output unlocked by a sprinkling of Buffs and a pinch of Momentum is an excellent candidate for Team Thrushwood.

Rating: A. Belle isn't an offensive powerhouse but they are so good at supporting the rest of your team that it doesn't matter and Thunder Rifle is good enough that you're not hurting for firepower anyway.

Dame Sapphire

Dame Sapphire - Sudden Conflict

Dame Sapphire is either Valrona's saviour or Valrona's doom and no-one will know which until it's too late to change that. With Queen Anastasia removed from the game board, Dame Sapphire has grown in influence and power. Despite the fact that the other 'faction' consists of a monster, an organ-harvesting witch and a vampire, I do wonder if Dame Sapphire is the true villain of this set...

 

Strengths

Bands of Belconnen is one of the few attacks that can be used multiple times in the same turn. It might only be a 2-dice ranged attack but it has the Area-keyword, so it hits everyone Dame Sapphire can see. The dream would be someone tagging Sapphire with a Body-type attack to trigger Corruptive Presence and land a Curse token on them for their trouble. Then in your turn you fire off a Bands of Belconnen buffed up with a bit of Momentum, they're re-rolling any successful dice and you might be able to sneak in a cheeky bit of damage that way.

Wicked Malediction gives you a decent melee attack followed by a Curse token, which is a good way to prevent that model from retaliating and bouncing something heavy on Dame Sapphire's noggin.

Overall, she reads as a very defensive character, punishing people for having a go at her or letting her get close.

 

Weaknesses

I don't love Fate's Promise. Spending an action to roll two dice to generate Momentum has an expected outcome of...*checks notes*...slightly more than one Momentum (this is because the consolation price for rolling zero successes on a test in Sudden Conflict is getting a Momentum). Many other characters in the game generate Momentum while doing other, productive things. So, I'm not a fan of Dame Sapphire's Momentum generation mechanics.

 

Good Partners

Dame Sapphire needs people to feed her Momentum for some cheeky Bands of Belconnen plays or even some buff tokens (Bless or whatever else is going around). Belle Thrushwood is definitely her best friend but you can also toss in Baron Halloway or a healer, like Ula to keep Sapphire alive after she baits people out with Curses. Arlax Jorl might also help, in providing some great offensive output to leverage off Cursed defence rolls as well as soaking hits for Sapphire as needed.

Rating: C. Dame Sapphire must have a foul mouth, what with all the cursing she does. I don't think she's got a great toolbox here. She's usable, not terrible, but she just doesn't have enough noteworthy abilities to recommend her above other characters.

The fate of fair Valrona hangs in the balance...(Sudden Conflict, photo courtesy of Musterkrux)

Wrap Up

And that’s Valrona, friends. Next time we’re going to provide some inspiration for painting your figures using a few of the techniques you might have seen in the photos accompanying these articles. We're talking Sin City-style painting, a bit of blending with Contrasts and even a cheeky bit of OSL (Object Source Lighting), all baked into a tiny package we like to call: How To Paint Everything (HTPE). Until next time!
