Tabletop Battles Reviews: Armageddon The Return of Yarrick Lore

April 25th, 2026 via Goonhammer
While the Ultramarines struggle to reclaim Ultramar, while Huron Blackheart and Prince Yriel duel over the riches of the Maelstrom, and while Perturabo and Kravek Morne unleash their ultimate plans to conquer the Imperium, war wages in this galaxy and nowhere is the war more dire than on the Imperial world of Armageddon.  But just *how* dire is the war, compared to the previous Wars for Armageddon 1) overall or 2) since the Heresy or even 3) in 10th Edition Lore?  And who is the Imperium fighting?  Let's Arma-geddit-on!

We’d like to thank Games Workshop for providing us with an advance copy of this lore for review purposes.

A Brief History of Armageddon

"Let the Imperial fleet see us bring Armageddon down on Ullanor!"  -Kubik, Fabricator General of the Adeptus Mechanicus, ca. M32.

The world at the center of this book has been the center of conflict between the Imperium, the Orks, and the Traitor Legions of Chaos since before Horus Lupercal and Angron fell.  During the Great Crusade, the world of Ullanor was the heart of Warboss Urlakk Urg's empire.  Billions of Orks threatened the borders of the nascent Imperium of Man, and The Emperor decreed that the Orks must be destroyed.  The Ultramarines and White Scars were sent to clear out the outlying worlds in the Ullanor system, while the Luna Wolves (then liveried in white and black armor) descended upon the planet, Horus Lupercal and Ezekyle Abaddon at the head.  After an epic battle in which a bunch of Sons of Horus died in heroic combat, Horus was victorious.  Urlakk Urg was dead, his Nobs were dead, and it was time for the Imperium to host a grand party.

Credit: Kevin Stillman

This party was known as the Triumph of Ullanor, and over a period of months the Mechanicum and Imperial Fists created a giant citadel and parade ground, decorated with Ork skulls.  Fourteen of the Legions and nine of the Primarchs (specifically including Angron and the World Eaters) attended, and at the conclusion the Emperor proclaimed Horus the Warmaster of the Imperium while he retired to Terra.  The Imperium then left Ullanor, and left a whole bunch of stuff behind.

1,500 years later, long after the Horus Heresy, the Orks returned to Ullanor.  During this time, the Orks fell under the leadership of a Warboss known as "The Beast" (or in the Orkish tongue, "Mag Uruk Thrakka") and six lieutenant Prime Orks.  These Prime-Orks were the “founders” of the various clans of Orks, with the biggest and baddest of these clans having taken the remains of Luna Wolves Space Marine armor as their symbols.  Hence why Orks use checkerboards.  

Credit: Robert "TheChirurgeon" Jones

These monsters were the size of Titans and they waged a devastating war against the Imperium.  This war even involved transporting a war moon to Sol System and threatening Terra itself.  The Imperial Fists were wiped out to a man, and even the attempted intervention of the Primarch Vulkan was not enough to bring this WAAAGH!!! to heel.   Eventually, the newly-formed Deathwatch and Sisters of Silence were able to force a captured Ork weirdboy to self-destruct and blow up all of the Prime-Orks.  In the aftermath, Imperial Fists Chapter Master Maximus Thane ordered the Fabricator General of the Adeptus Mechanicus to destroy Ullanor.  The Fabricator General, Kubik, had previously planned to move Mars out of the Sol System in the event the Orks destroyed Terra.  He repurposed the planetary teleportation technology to move Ullanor Prime to a different star system entirely so that they could harvest the planet's secrets and technology.  As a result, when this new world was resettled by the Imperium and the Mechanicus, the world had vast industrial hives to build war machines and raise vast armies.  Which Armageddon (presumably named in gest by the Mechanicus Explorators who "found" the world) would do for millennia.  

Credit: Robert "TheChirurgeon" Jones

Before the galaxy was sundered in the Great Rift, Armageddon was host to three major wars.  The First War of Armageddon was fought between the Space Wolves, Grey Knights, and the World Eaters.  Angron himself attacked Armageddon, and it took an entire company of Grey Knights to banish the Daemon Primarch.  In the aftermath, the Inquisition ordered the destruction of the entire population and Guard Regiments sent to fight the Twelfth Legion.  The Space Wolves objected to the destruction of the Guard, but the planet was depopulated and resettled.

Logan Grimnar. Credit: Rich Nutter

Centuries later, a new Ork WAAAGH!!! landed and began the Second War for Armageddon.  This WAAAGH!!! was led by a new warboss, Ghazghkull Mag Uruk Thraka, led a devastating assault that nearly conquered the world.  An Imperial Commissar, Sebastian Yarrick, overrode the planetary governor Herman von Strab (who was a total and complete scumbag) and summoned help from the wider Imperium.  Yarrick was relocated to Hades Hive, and led such a heroic defense that Ghazghkull themself was drawn to the battle.  This bought the defenders enough time for Dante, Marneus Calgar, and the Salamanders to arrive to turn the tide and drive the Orks away.  

Image credit: Games Workshop

Fifty Years later, Ghazghkull returned to avenge his pervious defeat in a Third War for Armageddon.  This time, Lord Commissar Yarrick fought alongside other heroes in defense of his world.  Chaplain Grimaldus and his Black Templars successfully defended Helsreach Hive before then heading to the Mannheim Gap to save what was left of the Celestial Lions Chapter from the Inquisition, while other Guard units and Space Marines defended other key locations on the planet.  Herman von Strab also came back, and sold out one of the Hive Cities to the Orks.  At the end of the war, Ghazghkull left the planet to go fight his Great WAAAGH!!! across the stars, while Yarrick and the Black Templars (under High Marshall Helbrecht) set off in hot pursuit.  An Astra Militarum Kill Team also eliminated the vile Herman von Strab.  

High Marshal Helbrecht. Credit: SRM

The Fourth War for Armageddon

While no one alive who does not live on Fenris or Titan really remembers Angron's First War for Armageddon, many of the artifacts left behind by Angron are not so forgetful.  With the coming of the Cicatrix Maledictum, these Chaos-Tainted relics have activated and now Khorne Daemons infest the northern continent of the planet.  The central locus of Chaos activity is "The Red Angel's Gate", a warp gate worsened by the Great Rift.  From there, the forces of Chaos spill out and continue their war against the Imperium.  Meanwhile, on the continent of Armageddon Secundus, Black Templar and Krieg forces attempting to secure the ruins of Hive Thoraddis before the World Eaters’ flagship Conqueror arrives and unleashes Heretic Astartes and their cultists.  They are successful in killing enough people and spilling enough blood to summon forth Angron, Daemon Primarch of the World Eaters.  

Grey Knights Army. Credit: Colin Ward

The Grey Knights' Prognosticators (last led by First War of Armageddon veteran Hyperion) dispatch Grand Master Rothwyr Morvans and send visions to Chapter Master Logan Grimnar and High Marshall Helbrecht of the threat to Armageddon.  This begins the Fourth War for Armageddon, in which the Eternal Crusader does battle against its corrupted sister ship, the Conqueror and Logan, Helbrecht, and the Grey Knights fight against an invading Angron.  The Grey Knights sought leftover relics from the First War for Armageddon so that they could banish Angron from the warzone.  They were able to temporarily banish Angron by detonating a bunch of Vortex Missiles left on the planet from the Ullanor days, but it took the Grey Knights using the remnants of Angron's shattered sword to destabilize the Red Angel's Gate and finally get Angron off of Armageddon.  

So the planet remains a war torn mess following the opening of the Great Rift, it's grievous wounds worsened by the return of a Daemon Primarch and fighting between the Space Wolves (who want the citizens of Armageddon defended) and the Black Templars (who don't care).  

Armageddon Besieged

Ghazghkull Thraka. Credit: Corrode

The book begins on the doomed world of Icaria, where the Lord Commissar did fight The Beast.  And despite the assistance of great heroes, the battle's outcome was not in doubt: Ghazghkull won.  His power claw grabbed Yarrick, cracked his bones, and then headbutted the old man.  Ghaz decided this was good enough, had a big laugh, and left to go elsewhere.  What was left of Yarrick was spirited away by a Space Wolves Wolf Priest and rebuilt aboard the warship the Ice Troll.  When Yarrick asks why he was healed, the Priest tells Yarrick that he was "more important to the fate of Armageddon than he realized", and returned to the world that made him famous.  And not a moment too soon, for Ork fleets had returned to blockade Armageddon.  

Since the Third War for Armageddon, Ghazghkull has been knocking about the universe fighting and smashing everything in his way.  His Great Waaagh! was starting to become a katamari, picking up most of the other named Ork characters (Boss Snikrot, Wazdakka, Nazdreg Ug Urdgrub, and the Overfiend of Octarius) and rolling over entire subsectors.  It was then that Ghazghkull came to realize a few things:
  1. Things were not going well in the galaxy, because it was getting crazier and someone else might break the galaxy before he did.
  2. His Waaagh!!! was too big, and he needed to spend more time managing it.
  3. Conquering the galaxy was taking too long.
So Ghaz decided to head back to Armageddon and smash everything there.  But Ghazghkull, having been decapitated by Ragnar Blackmane before, realized he was going to have to hang back and spend his time directing forces all throughout the Armageddon subsector versus just jumping onto the planet and smashing it to pieces.  He also had a new toy: the Mega Tellyshokka.  Built by himself, Nazdreg, and "Orkimedes", this system was ten arrays built into ten different Kroozers that would build a hole through the Warp.  With this setup, Ghazghkull's Great Waaagh! could teleport entire armadas and then flee.  When Ghaz jumped into Armageddon, they displaced an entire star fortress, a moon, and a planet within the system.  

The Season of Fire was ending, and the Season of Shadows was beginning.  However, Angron's invasion had scattered the Orks, and the Imperium had been reinforced with new heroes: Canoness Preceptor Mercia and the enthroned Dogmata Superior, Intranzia Fraye, had arrived with several preceptories of the Order of Our Martyred Lady, the mysterious Inquisitor Ynighoh Kroyle of the Ordo Xenos, and even a new planetary governor, Agmund von Ruger.  These new heroes convened the Council of Armageddon in order to plan for a decisive strike to purge the Orks from Armageddon once and for all!  The Astra Militarum, under General Tybault Locke (who once served under General Kurov, he of the 8th Edition Aquila relic), was not convinced this was a feasible plan and could blow up in everyone's face.  

Credit: Dan Boyd

Then entered Commissar Graves, who proceeded to rip everyone a new one for tolerating the Orks on Armageddon.  So Locke began planning an offensive.  There were two main Ork strongholds: Hive Tempestora and the Equatorial Jungles.  The former was ruled by Warboss Ugrokk Glitztoof, while the jungles were led by scattered but numerous and cranky Ork tribes.  Locke opted for the latter choice, and broke his armies into mechanized purgation corps of light armor and transports with flamers and chemical weapons.  At first, this strategy seemed to work: between AdMech assistance, Cadian and Krieg infantry, and Armageddon's transports, some territory was reclaimed.   Then the skies above Armageddon began to light up with an aurora of sickly green light, and an Ork Armada jumped out of warp in near-Armageddon orbit.  With the WAAAGH!!! seemingly everywhere all at once, the situation was dire.  The Ice Wolf, stuck in orbit, was unable to contact Imperial Command on Armageddon or send an astropathic request for assistance.  Yarrick was able to convince the Wolves to spare him a transport to travel to Hive Volcanus, while the Wolves would secure food and water supplies for the planets beleagaured defenders. 

Credit: Kevin Stillman

Wazdakka's Speedwaaagh!!!

The first Ork to arrive on Armageddon from this armada was none other than Wazdakka Gutsmek.  Irritated and itchy, Wazdakka hopped on his bike while still aboard his landing craft and vroomed vroomed  his way onto the planet.  Whereever he drove, Kults of Speed would gather and turn whatever was under them into a dragstrip.  But Wazdakka had a job to do, and Wazdakka was not running this show.  Wazdakka's bigga’ boss had impressed upon him (via Gork's Klaw) that he was required to encircle Hive Volcanus.  And so Wazdakka did his job, overrunning Krieg defensive fortifications and minefields.  Indeed, Wazdakka and his Speedwaaagh!!! attacked the massive fortifications defending the Manheim Gap, and with both sides unleashing artillery of all sorts (surface, air, and even orbital), Wazdakka zoomed on through and was able to use his bike's Warp Drive to teleport past various obstacles.  The Imperium's Liberation Line failed, and Hive Volcanus was in deep deep doo-doo.  

Wazdakka Gutsmek. Credit: 40khamslam.

General Locke had marshalled his forces to face Wazdakka's rampage, but as it was traveling to the Mannheim Gap, his relief force was quickly surrounded!  Wazdakka’s blitz was just too intense for even the redoubtable defenses between the Gap and Hive Volcanus to hold back the oncoming Orks.  The defenders of Armageddon were besieged, being cut down as their defensive perimeter tightened and tightened.  Only the Sisters of Battle were having anything resembling success, but they would not be enough.  General Locke needed a miracle.  

With a roar, a Space Wolves Thunderhawk arrived, followed by Valkyrie transports deploying drop troops of the Tempestus Scions.  From the Thunderhawk, three Space Marines interceded into the conflict.  But everyone's attention was on the individual they were escorting: Sebastian Yarrick returned!   Yarrick's presence and fury was enough to singlehandedly turn the tide: the Orks lost their nerve at the arrival of a herald of death whose evil eye promised only doom; while the Imperials redoubled their efforts - they were soldiers of The Emperor and they would not be found wanting under the eye of Commissar Yarrick!  With Yarrick returned, he and Locke were able to save a third of the relief force and reposition to Hive Volcanus.  Wazdakka's bike was busted, and he was slowed.  

Credit: Crab-Stuffed Mushrooms

Yarrick's return had bought the Imperial defenders time to regroup and reinspire.  After Wazdakka's Speedwaaagh!!! smashed the entire Imperial force in the Mannheim Gap to bits (Wazdakka's grabba dragga shooting out to ensnare the lead Sisters Canoness and drag her along behind Big Revva for a bit), the encirclement of Hive Volcanus was complete.

Within the Council for Armageddon, Yarrick announced it was time to put the Imperium's final contingencies into place.  He had with him a gold-plated Servo Skull, the Pharus Horrifica.  Crafted from the cranium of a Master Astropath, this skull would be able to focus and hone the entire astropathic choir's ritual of sending and ensure it cut through the warp turbulence and would take priority over every other distress signal.  Yarrick was going to use the entire astropathic choir of the Hive to send a distress signal to alert every Space Marine Chapter, sector commander, and other Imperial force.  The Council agreed, and Yarrick went off to send the signal with Governor Von Ruger.

Commissar Graves - credit: keewa

Commissar Graves took the opportunity to vent her frustrations at the fact that the Imperium just had it's butt handed to it by the Orks.  Graves could have looked at the individual who demanded that the Orks be purged by any means necessary and demanded the Astra Militarum on the planet divide its strength in a purgation campaign heedless of any possibility the Orks could regroup.  Instead, she looked at the person who had considered such a course of action reckless, she had bullied into pulling it off, and then fought valiantly when Graves' error in judgment nearly wiped out his relief force.

BLAM!

Commissar Graves then had Locke's corpse dragged from the council chamber, and appointed Marshal Gorman of the Death Korps of Krieg to run the Astra Militarum.  He was a "coldly calculating commander" who appealed to her "own callous sensibilities", and his mathematical methods were blunt and unimaginative but that would be what could win the war for Armageddon.

Unfortunately for Yarrick, when he and the Governor went to rally the Astropathic Chior, the door was shut.  When the door was unlocked, the Astropathic Circular Chamber had been smashed up.  Machinery was shattered, corpses within the water, and the astropaths at their stations hacked to death.  Ork Kommandos had gotten in and out and left a message: "No Escape".

Kommandos. Credit: Rockfish

Situation Normal, Armageddon Facin' Usual  

While Hive Volcanus could hold out for weeks or even months, but without a way to send an astropathic signal via the Pharus Horrifica, the Imperium was doomed.  While four of the Hives were assigned astropathic relays, Hives Helsreach and Infernus were cut off by the equatorial jungles, and Hive Volcanus' choir was butchered.  This gave Yarrick only one option: escape to Hive Death Mire.  

Yarrick and his force would have to break out of the Ork encirclement of Volcanus onto Volcanus Highway, outrun Wazdakka, and pass through the area around Ugrokk Glitztoof's Hive Tempestora.  Yarrick would need a distraction, and Commissar Graves would lead it.  This force would not be a fast, mobile force.  Instead it would be a "blunt anvil" and smash into Wazdakka's Speedwaaagh!  The Sisters of Battle and the Astra Militarum forces retreating from the fall of the Mannheim Gap were reformed into understrength battalions, and assigned to Commissar Graves' strike force.  Yarrick's force had all of the new vehicles releasing alongside this book, Leman Russes, and House Terryn Knights.  The armies assembled, the gates of Hive Volcanus swung open and Commissar Graves rode forward in her Centaur RSV Vigilance, backed by Rogals Dorn and other Centaur RSVs. The Death Korps of Krieg led the way for the poor doomed infantry, reoccupying previously abandoned trenches and rallying the less motivated (i.e. suicidal) troops behind them.  This attack caught the Speedwaaagh! off guard, and they had initial trouble responding.  Indeed, Commissar Graves was able to drive forward and hit a bunch of Orks with her sword.  The battle won, the Sisters of Battle moved forward to reinforce the Guard's positions and offer them motivation, both positive and negative.  They would not have to wait long before the Speedwaaagh! would return and smash into their lines.

Battle Sisters. Credit: Corrode

The light vehicles of the Speedwaaagh! were smashed to pieces by the tanks, while the Imperial cavalry pitched Ork bikers from their saddles.  Ork Battlewagons, however, charged through the carnage with wrecking balls that pulped sponson weapons and tank commanders.  But the Speedwaaagh! had already committed their fatal error: their Trukks and Battlewagons stopped, their flanks were exposed and concealed Imperial armored unit unleashed fire into the Ork column.  Graves had dismounted and was personally leading, fighting alongside, and rallying the troops against waves of Ork Boys, while Dogmata Superior Fraye and the Order of Our Martyred Lady sung wrathful prayers as they blasted the Orks with bolter fire.  They all fought bravely, but there were always more Orks and Wazdakka was starting to get free of the carnage and resume his blitz.  Graves knew that it was time to pack it up and make a tactical withdrawal before the lines completely collapsed.  Hopping back into the Gravesmobile, she ordered what Imperial units that could return to Volcanus return to Volcanus.  Those unable to retreat were ordered to fight to the last.  While the Sisters of Our Martyred Lady were not going to accept martyrdom just yet, the Death Korps of Krieg (Marshal Gorman among them) were.

Less than an eighth of Graves' force survived the battle to retreat back to the Hive, but their mission was accomplished.  

Death Race Volcanus

With Wazdakka's Speedwaaagh! distracted at the north, Deathskull Speedboss Grizsnag decided to do some lootin' and racing on the other side of Hive Volcanus Hive.  The southeastern back of the Hive was not too heavily defended, so everything was fun and games until the Sainted Gate swung open.  Out rolled Hippogriffs, Centaur RSVs, Leman Russes and Rogals Dorn, Imperial Knights of Houses Terryn and Hawkshroud, Adeptus Mechanicus transports, and two Warhound Titans.  At the head was the legend reborn: Commissar Yarrick and the Fortress of Arrogance!  Warboss Grizsnag was quickly immolated, and it was time for Yarrick's desperate battle down the burning highway.  There would be no fuel breaks, potty breaks, or water breaks - the few gas stations between Volcanus and Death Mire were in Ork hands.  It was going to be a running battle across the wasteland, and even Yarrick's air cover was going to need to refuel in the planet's stratosphere.  When Wazdakka found out that the big scrap he was in was a feint for an epic death race, he was one very angry Ork.  At this point, the narrative draws attention to the fact that the weather on Armageddon was going completely off the rails, and Yarrick headed into the storm.  

Baneblade. Credit: Rockfish

We get some small character moments from Yarrick here: Unlike Commissar Graves, who is a "shoot them to motivate them, it's never my fault it's only their heresy" Commissar, Yarrick cares about the lives of the people he has to sacrifice.  His methods of motivation are to offer trust, encouragement, and utterly impossible duty.  And even though the people he sends to his death are aware his orders are noble sacrifices, the soldiers go willingly.  

Unfortunately, Yarrick's column eventually ran into an obstacle it could not simply drive through: an under-construction Scrap Fortress, cutting the road off at a narrow passage.  It was surrounded by floodlights and artillery, with towers and bunkers and filled with Boyz and armor.  There was also an incoming radioactive dust storm approaching from the south.  The only thing the Imperials could do was to reform the convoy and continue to head through the Fortress: they may not be able to smash through it easy, so they would smash through it hard.  As the column came to the gates, the Orks opened fire and the storm hit.  

The battle was as tough as it was fast.  The Orks instantly destroyed one of the Warhound Titans, but the Imperium were able to destroy several of the Stompas.  Many other Knights fell (one Stompa managed to waste three of them in one swing), but the Imperium was able to break through and continue on their death race to Death Mire.  And there it was in front of him.  The Hive was besieged and beleaguered, but titanic Aquila banners still fluttered, giving proof through the night that the Imperium was still there.  Yarrick made contact with the Hive, where Inquisitor Kroyle and a Purgation Corps group waited.  

And that's when Wazdakka caught up with Yarrick.

The Imperium threw everything they had in order to punch a hole into the Death Mire access tunnel.  Wazdakka and his teleporting Speed Freaks, however, had successfully jumped to directly in front of the tunnel and were racing to meet Yarrick's column.  Even the mighty Fortress of Arrogance was suffering from the sheer amount of firepower unleashed by the Speed Freaks, while warbikes and AFVs exploded in the crossfire.  The Hound of Hades, the last remaining Warhound Titan, stepped in to give Yarrick cover and present a more tempting target for Wazdakka Gutsmek.  Wazdakka took the bait, and raced around the feet of the Warhound.  Grabba dragga, however, had manage to shatter enough of the Titan's ankle hydraulics and motors that it unbalanced the Titan and gave the Orks the opportunity to fire on the damaged ankle.  This caused the Warhound to collapse and explode, giving Wazdakka his second Engine Kill.  But the Hound of Hades successfully fulfilled its duty to The Omnissiah, and Yarrick and his tanks had managed to reach Hive Death Mire!  Wazdakka took this as the opportunity to go off and have fun elsewhere.

Wazdakka Gutsmek. Credit: Corrode

In orbit of Armageddon was the massive krooza Kill Wrecka, where Ghazkull Mag Uruk Thraka oversaw his planetwide campaign.  While the Prophet of the WAAAGH!!! longed to join in the fighting, he was forced to remain in orbit in order to keep the overall campaign on track.  After all, the Blood Axe finker-boyz would stop listening to their Goff bosses if he left.  But then two of the Boyz told him of a big black soopa-wagon leadin' a Humie convoy out of Hive Volcanus.  Ghazkull was elated that his best enemy was there for fighting, but was also concerned that Yarrick would manage to reach the Death Mire astropaths and send out his signal to contact the Space Marines.  Ghaz could not be having that, so he decided to blast Yarrick from orbit when Yarrick emerged from the tunnel.  

Meanwhile, Inquisitor Kroyle is snooping around a refinery complex towards the other end of the tunnel with a force of militia and 50 Tempestus Scions.  He's having a jolly old time sniping Ork Beastbosses when Yarrick's convoy arrives and the Fortress of Arrogance gets sniped from orbit by Ghaz's cannon. 

Inquisitor Kroyle. Credit: Rich Nutter

Kroyle is initially in despair, because the Fortress of Arrogance is burning.  Is hope dead?  Nope, Yarrick had quietly transferred to a Chimera beforehand.  He is alive and now they are less than five miles from the Command Spire.  The Orks are coming.

Kroyle's troops may be mere hive militia, but now they're Yarrick's honor guard and just saw him survive certain death AGAIN.  There can be no warriors more fanatical or terrifying.  Even the mighty Ork hordes are having to run from the troops as they race to the central spire.  But the Orks are also starting to swarm towards the central spires and multiple WAAAGHs! are already raging within Death Mire.  Yarrick and Kroyle's forces dwindle as they try and sneak and fight their way through the ruined Hive.  The last of the Tempestus Scions make their final stand covering Yarrick's flight up into Calgar Tower (1), and eventually Yarrick, Kroyle, and a mere 15 Hive Militia reach the command spire.  Presenting the Pharus Horrifica to the Hive's Astropathic Choir, they are aware of the cost that they will pay in sending the message (they'll get burned alive).  Every other Weirdboy and Astropath in the star system gets fried by the signal, many perishing from the shock.  But the signal is sent.  

The Heroes of Armageddon

Somewhat redundantly after the exciting narrative portion of our book, GW then gives us the stories of the dramatic personae of Armageddon.  

Commissar Thenia Graves 

As noted above, Commissar Thenia Graves is the single scariest human being on Armageddon.  Straight out of 5th Edition, Commissar Graves'd approach is, "That of an unflinching instrument of martial justice, carrying out executions that strengthen the ability of the Imperium's armies to fight effectively by removing those she deems incompetent."  

The book then deconstructs that line by demonstrating the depths of Graves' failures of leadership.  "Once Graves has safely left a warzone, it has also been observed that instances of officers willing to show initiative have plummeted."  When one decorated and popular officer was trying to fight the Leagues of Votann on Bleak, Graves got mad that the officer was not fighting in a single headlong offensive.  Graves tied the officer to the front of a tank, and then sent the tank and the Guard straight into the Kin.  The Imperium suffered horrific casualties, and Graves ripped the Colonel's corpse from the tank and blasted her head with a bolt gun.  

"It is not Graves' role to strategise or to command.  Such duties belong to the Imperial Guard's regimental officers and generals...nor...does she seek the power to wield armies, except where duty compels here".  However, she does it anyway and relies on her Right Hand to take over in leading the army after she murders the entire command leadership.  

Commissar Graves - credit: keewa

The Imperium sent Graves to Armageddon in the wake of the Fourth War for Armageddon in order to root out heretics and identify holdouts of loyal Guardsmen on Armageddon Secundus.  While within the ruins of Hive Thoraddis, Graves reviewed surveillance footage.  She thought it was low worth, showing shadowy figures moving in loping, jerking runs with incomprehensible audio.  She watched the video again and again, however, in that she could understand what caused the disloyalty within the hive.  On her eighth viewing, she saw something else.  Something gigantic and half-seen.  Ever since, she's been even more angry.  

Dogmata Superior Intranzia Fraye

Intranzia Fraye - credit: keewa

She is effectively the head Chaplain for the Order of Our Martyred Lady, so she is in charge of making sure the rituals and ideology of the Adepta Soroitas are followed precisely.  She does not tolerate failure from Sisters, and she does not tolerate foes, period.  She has "notoriously scan" in her capacities for mercy and forgiveness, and is quick to condemn her sisters into Mortifiers.  Fraye bestrides the battlefield in her "armoured walking engine", the Throne of Blame.  This is an ancient relic of the Order, where she gives angry sermons while unleashing her fury upon the friends and foes.  Her melee weapon, the Mace of Saint Praxedes, is named for the first martyr from the Second Tyrannic War and has disruptor coils from the Dark Age of Technology.  She uses it to beat things to death.  

Inquisitor Kroyle

Ynighoh Kroyle is a radical, eccentric, flamboyant, and cold-hearted alien hunter.  He does not work with sentient Xenos species, but he is willing to steal their tools so he can hunt and kill them.  He seeks out the biggest and smartest alien beasts and war leaders in order to shoot them in the head.  While he is an egotistical murderer, he does have the good sense to let other Imperium forces know about the beings he has hunted and killed so that they can hunt and kill them, too.  

His weapon is a Jindarii tox-cycler, an alien weapon that shoots bony, poisonous darts that break up when they hit a target.  These fragments send a signal back to the gun, which reformulates the poison the darts are coated in to make them more lethal.

Kroyle is fairly certain the Garralisk is the last of its kind, because he spent months killing the rest of the species.  It's pretty good as a mount, where he keeps it drugged up and sniffing out his prey.  

Hippogriff AFV

Cadian Hippogriff AFV. Credit: Rich Nutter

It's adorable.  But it's also an armored fighting vehicle that does armored fighting vehicle things.  We covered it and the Centaur RSV here.  

Centaur RSV

It's a truck with big tires to drive across the battlefields of the Indomitus Crusade.  I look at this model and I see an Ork Trukk waiting to be looted.  

Commissar Sebastian Yarrick

In contrast to Commissar Graves, Commissar Yarrick believes in leadership, discipline, and the Emperor of Mankind.  It turns out that a Commissar is a far better example of duty and inspiration when he engages in daring acts of courage and bravery rather than simply murdering people for looking at him funny.  

When he was thought dead, an interstellar market opened up in selling relics from his body and uniform.  Others spread rumors that Yarrick was a saint, leading a crusade Somewhere Else, or was now on the Senatorum Imperialis.  Mostly the idea from the Departmento Munitorum was to use Yarrick's death to inspire the soldiery of the Astra Militarum, though.  

He received the Pharum Horrifica from High Marshal Helbrecht of the Black Templars before they parted ways.  When Yarrick had been returned to life by the Space Wolves, Yarrick was pretty surprised that they cared about him.  Furthermore, Yarrick did not know why the Space Wolves thought that they also had some sort of connection to Armageddon.  

Armageddon Has Fallen

Orks are swarming into the entire Armageddon subsector.  Those that can't fit onto the planet itself are swarming into every ship, facility, and other planet in the Armageddon system.  Those that can't fit into the star system are attacking other worlds.  Hive Volcanus is besieged.  Hive Death Mire is teetering.  Yarrick cannot stop to breathe and rest: the Imperial defenders need a hero to inspire and lead and they need a Commissar to exemplify discipline and duty.  

Weeks later, the situation continued to deteriorate.  Even Yarrick was beginning to despair.  That's when Yarrick was handed a dataslate:  Hive Acheron had fallen.  The Orks dropped an asteroid on it, and then when the Hive's defenses destroyed the asteroid it split into roks and deployed all sorts of Orks.  The last transmission noted Ghazghkull himself had landed.  This threatened to finally break the legend.  Ghaz was not coming after him (even though Yarrick was baiting him!), he was going to fight Yarrick on his terms and his terms alone.  

But at Yarrick's darkest moment within the command strategium of Death Mire, light appeared on every hololith and auspex.  He knew those signals.  

Thunderhawk Gunships.  Drop Pods.  

He received a transmission from a Space Marine, consisting of one word:  "Imperator".  

Credit: Corrode

Final Thoughts

It took me a minute to realize what this story was telling because of the way it was being told.  But once you strip away the overly grimdark technogothic exterior of 40K from the tale, you can see it:  Yarrick is trying to place a pre-order for the 11th Edition Starter Box and the Orks are trying to stop him.  And once he places the preorder, it takes weeks for him to arrive to add the new Space Marines to his army.  

But let’s discuss the overly grimdark technogothic exterior, shall we?  Because the story in this book absolutely blows away the other three End-of-Tenth campaign books.  We have two fun and contrasting viewpoint characters: Commissar Sebastian Yarrick, who exemplifies the reason why the Imperial Guard need and rely on Commissars, and Commissar Thenia Graves, who exemplifies the reason why the Imperial Guard’s Commissars are a terrible idea in implementation.  Graves in particular I found fascinating.  The narrative deconstructs the 1d4Chan-style Commissar, showing that they absolutely do exist in-universe and why they exist and thrive.  GW also shows how this behavior corrupts the Imperium and weakens the Imperium.  Graves was already dangerously cruel, corrupt, and insane *before* she decided to start doomscrolling and fell to Chaos.   The other Imperium characters, Inquisitor Kroyle and Sister Fraye, also fall into the 5th edition archetype.  Fraye is clearly a reimagining/reinterpretation of Inquisitor Fyodor Karamazov, being effectively a chaplain in the context of the religious crazy faction of 40K.  In the context of this story we don’t get to see her do too much in the way of interesting things, though.  Likewise, Inquisitor Kroyle does not have a lot to do in this story either.  He shows up to help Yarrick out then vanishes.  We are told that he’s an Imperial Weirdo’s Weirdo, but we don’t get a feel for his character at this time.  I suspect that eventually Black Library will get around to writing a story about him, probably pairing him against Trazyn or Belisarius Cawl.

Of our two main Ork characters, we only get limited characterization:  They’re Kunnin' but Brutal.  While Wazdakka is the main focus, being the big Ork model for this release, his characterization feels like an extension of Ghazghkull’s characterization.  Ghaz is thinking ahead and learning from this mistakes, and coming up with plans.  Wazdakka is likewise Kunnin’ but Brutal, so he can see the sorts of plans Ghaz is formulating and understand why he should execute those plans for reasons other than “Ghaz will beat the crap out of me”.  And we see them both use the various bits of technology that they’ve scrounged up in previous stories to enact their master plan.  

If I had to say something I did not care for in this book, it’s a continuation of something that has started to bother me since 500 Worlds: Titus.  I do not mind the Imperium taking a loss in one of these books.  As Rob has noted, narratively we need them.  Indeed, I do not mind the Imperium taking a loss in three of the four books (with them just not being there in Book 2).  What I’m not super-thrilled with is how the Imperium is taking a loss in these books, which is that the Xenos or Chaos simply have more bodies and logistics to win.  Individually, I’d be fine with any one of these three factions winning that way.  After all, Necrons teleport in, Orks come from all over for the biggest fights, and Perturabo is a legendary logician.  But to do them all at the same time is a little much for me.  

However, the Return of Yarrick is a love letter to the story, characters, faction, and spirit of the 5th Edition of Warhammer 40,000 but with modern models, modern model sensibilities, and writing.  It’s a good tale and I enjoyed it the most out of the four books.
