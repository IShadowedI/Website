Thoughts

Ross’ plan here is to infiltrate a ton of Kroot to block any scout moves or infiltrating options Andy might have, then go first and put Andy directly into Kroot jail. The Kroot Hunting Pack is a massive help in this matchup, where giving the otherwise t-shirted bird aliens a 5+ invulnerable save against ranged attacks is a massive durability buff against an army which otherwise might be able to wipe them out using AP-1 Pyreblasters, and a 6+ Feel No Pain on top of that with a Flesh Shaper (this can improve to a 5+ once they kill something in melee). If things go well for Ross, he’ll pull off some nasty turn 1 charges, putting Andy’s army in jail and holding them there for multiple turns with Kroot Carnivores while the Krootox Rampagers to come in and and kill the heavier targets. Nearly everything in the army has Scouts 7″, making it trivially easy to get turn 1 charges off with the entire army if Andy moves forward at all.

But there’s the rub: You don’t actually want to get off turn 1 charges with most of this army – five marines can very much kill a unit of Kroot Hounds in melee, so you want to use them less as an actual melee threat and more as a cheap, portable barrier, blocking movement to the middle of the table and forcing an opponent to shoot them. Shooting them doesn’t move you any closer to objectives though, so if you weren’t already standing on them and don’t have anything to charge, you won’t be standing on them a turn later, either. That’s the ideal situation, since nothing in Ross’ army can credibly threaten the Land Raider.

For Andy, the key here is those Infernus marines – being +1 Strength into T3 targets with a Pyreblaster puts them on a 2+ to wound and being able to throw out 5D6 shots at a time is critical to chewing through the 100+ models Ross has on the table. An average activation will push out 15 wounds and that should be enough to kill 10 Kroot. Rinse and repeat a few times while getting support from the vehicles and holding primary and this is very doable. The primary problem is just bodies: Andy has to kill a lot of Kroot early and prevent a battle of attrition – one he’s likely to lose as his melee output is more anemic than you normally expect to see in marine armies.

The big problem is that Andy can only fire Overwatch once per turn and that won’t be enough to handle the sheer volume of bird aliens coming across the table. The Kroot can win the activation game and rack up secondary points easily while stealing primary with OC 2 Karnivores and blocking Andy’s movement. Andy won’t have any problem scoring “Kill one” or “Kill More on Purge”, but may struggle with hold more. There are a lot of armies geared toward killing heavier infantry which will struggle into Kroot Hunting Pack; Andy’s Forgefather’s Seekers list isn’t one of them.

This is where the mission helps Andy, as all he needs to do is hold a single objective – his home – to score Hold One, and scoring kill primary VP will be much easier. Andy is likely to score Kill More early on but may struggle to close the scoring gap on later turns, especially as numbers dwindle and scoring easy kill more points becomes impossible just because the number of targets aren’t there. That said, a 4-point primary gap isn’t the end of the world and the game is likely to stay tied or close on Primary. It’s a recipe for a close game that’s likely to come down to Secondary draws or who can force a single 12-8 primary turn.

Result

Ross blocked Andy in his Deployment Zone and put the rest of his army on the line, then Scouted everything forward 7″. He spent the first two rounds deftly blocking Andy in his Deployment Zone and keeping him off all three objectives at mid table, putting him on Hold One most of the game. That 12-8 primary turn happened on round 3 and ended up making the difference – going second was the advantage Ross needed, letting him match Andy on kills in the third round.

Thoughts

The star of the show here are the Infernus Marines, who very much get off on having S6 pyreblasters to work with and the ability to re-roll Wound rolls with a boost from Vulkan. Adrax and the Bladeguard give it some melee punch and it has enough mid-range shooting to handle 3W marine targets.

Archetype

Army of Faith Sisters

Thoughts

The Army of Faith build is a particular perversion of Goonhammer author TwoHorse, who has been on that wagon for months, extolling its virtues alongside the idea of running heavy bolter Paragons. The ability to double up on Acts of Faith which each unit can just create some tremendous bullshit, especially from the Retributors who can use it to punch through a ton of damage if you fail some saves against their multi-meltas. The Immolators here strip cover and the triple Castigators make good use of that while also triggering +1 AP against a target they hit, letting the army combo its way up against bigger threats.
