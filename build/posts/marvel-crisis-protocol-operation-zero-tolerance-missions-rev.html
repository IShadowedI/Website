Marvel Crisis Protocol: Operation Zero Tolerance Missions Revealed

April 27th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Another year brings a new set of crises to enjoy! This article will cover some of the newly leaked Operation Zero Tolerance missions for Marvel Crisis Protocol. Once better photos become available they will be added in.

Some Terminology

  • King of the Hill - These secure crisis require your characters to stand in a particular spot to score them. If both players contest the same objective it can cause neither player to score it which can in some cases slow the game.
  • Pay to Flip - These secures require you to pay to interact with them to give you a chance to control them. Usually once you control them you are free to leave until your opponent interacts to take them off you. Teams that have additional power early can take advantage of this as they don't have to choose between picking up an extract and flipping a point.
  • Max (x) VPs - The maximum number of victory points you can score in a round for a crisis. The available Victory points on the board ultimately dictates how quickly or slowly a game will go. If you consistently score 4 victory points per round you will get to 16 and win the game after 4 rounds. Scoring 1 less at 3 per round would cause the game to take a full 6 rounds to score out instead so even 1 more victory point available can dictate the pace of the game.
  • Asset or Civilian - The type of extract being used for the crisis. A small but significant number of rules interact with only one of these. Examples would be the Inhuman's card Terrigenesis and Namor's ability to force you to drop extracts.
  • Attrition - The playstyle of trying to daze and or KO your opponent to prevent them scoring in order to win the game in the long run.
  • Source Extracts - A new style of extract where there is a fixed source where you initially pick up extracts from. In the cleanup phase extracts you have picked up go back to the source and you have to pick them up all over again.
  • Secure Zone - A new style of secure where there is an arc on the secure affecting a specific part of the battlefield. When being placed the player placing the token can rotate it in any direction.
  • Limited - Characters can only hold one limited objective token.

Extracts

Evidence of Experimental Soldiers Exposed (19)

Evidence of Experimental Soldiers crisis for Marvel Crisis Protocol

Max 3 VPs - Asset - Source

There are 3 source extracts down the middle of the battlefield. The gimmick with this one is that if you are holding the extract you can reroll a defence dice.

The effect is helpful for scoring teams as it gives a little extra survivability however the shape and the low number of victory points on the table mean that this crisis might favor attrition teams.

As this is a source extract your characters effectively drop the extracts in each cleanup phase and have to go back to the center of the table to pick them up again. As the sources themselves are so central this will encourage a brawl in the middle.

Characters with the hit and run superpower might at least be able to run in, pick up the extract and hit and run out of too much danger. Ultimately the best tactic here is likely to be taking strong turret characters who can just stand on the source picking up every turn and double attacking anyone who comes into range.

Sentinel Schematics Sabotaged! (17)

Sentinel Schematics Sabotaged Credit Atomic Mass Games

Max 4 VPs - Asset - Source

Another Source extract with centrally focused sources. This crisis is the new version of hammers as when you are holding the extract and attacking a character within range 2 you can add 1 dice to the attack roll.

Once again as a centrally focused source extract this crisis favors a brawl in the center of the table. The range 2 limitation of the additional dice means that charge characters with range 2 attacks like Wolverine will love this crisis.

Salvaged Supplies Fuel Resistance Efforts (18)

Salvaged Supplies Fuel Resistance Efforts Credit Atomic Mass Games

Max 4 VPs - Asset

This will be an interesting puzzle to pick apart as it will be difficult to pick these extracts up early. They are worth 2 victory points each but you can only pick them up if another non grunt allied character is contesting them. Additionally you can't pass them between characters with something like the Kingpin leadership as they drop instead.

These effects combined mean that you won't be picking these up with your first activation which is very different from what we are used to. The only way I can think of to abuse this would be to use Sin and Crossbones activating one after another, which would be a strong effect...except for the fact you have to take Sin and Crossbones.

Ultimately this is another extract that likely favors an attrition team as it makes target priority easier for them as fewer characters will be holding the extract. Even if it is worth 2 Victory points they can daze a character and then place the extract somewhere where an opponents character isn't contesting it. This means that even if they themselves don't pick it up their opponent won't be able to either.

The stipulation that they drop when moved means that characters like Black Cat who can steal extracts instead can only force a drop. Sometimes this might be enough if you can drop the extract in a place both you an a non-grunt ally are contesting to then pick it up.

Surprise Assault! Mutant Homes Destroyed (16)

Surprise Assault Mutant Homes Destroyed Credit Atomic Mass Games

Max 4 VPs - Civilian - Source

This extract has a very different shape to what we have seen before though ultimately it isn't too different from the others. A source extract that is centrally focused. Out of the source extracts it is at least slightly more spread out which helps scoring teams slightly but being a centrally focused source extract will still be good for attrition teams.

16 Threat is the lowest threat value we have in this rotation and historically at low threat values attrition teams can bring a big baddie that other teams struggle to deal with.

There is some funny wording on this crisis: "During the Cleanup Phase remove all Mutants from the game." Sooo don't play X-men on this crisis I guess...

Secures

X-Men Infiltrate Secret Weapons Facility (17)

X-Men Infiltrate Secret Weapons Facility Credit Atomic Mass Games

Max 5 VPs - Pay to Flip

This is the first crisis we've looked at so far with a secure zones. In fact out of these secure crisis it is the only crisis with zones so if you don't want to replace your tokens try not to play this crisis.

The arcs face inward for the north and south secure and outward for the east and west secure. This crisis is a pay to flip using a characters energy defence and you score a victory point for each one you secure in the clean up phase. You get 1 additional victory point if you control 2 or more that have at least 1 enemy character in their arc. In the cleanup phase each player places up to 1 grid they control within range 1 of its current position. They've clarified on the rules that when you place a zone you can rotate it so that the arc is facing whichever direction you want.

All combined this crisis will be an interesting one. There are a maximum of 5 points available which is quite high. They start in a diamond range 4+2 in from the sides however as they can be moved by the end of the game they could end up closer or further apart than this.

I can see teams like Guardians of the Galaxy favoring this secure, they generally have high energy defence to pay to flip this and enemy characters will end up in a good enough shooting gallery if they come in to flip it.

Power Overload! Factory Goes up in Flames (19)

Power Overload Factory Goes Up in Flames Credit Atomic Mass Games

Max 5 VPs - King of the Hill

Another crisis with a new shape. This asymmetrical diamond will be interesting for deployment as if you want to contest the central point closer to your opponent you will be putting your character in danger. You can score an additional victory point if you secure 2 or more conduits and at least 2 of them are not within 5 of one another. Effectively if you do manage to get one of your opponent's secures you might get an extra victory point for your trouble. Kingpin's criminal syndicate will like this secure as on round 1 some of their difficult to kill characters could dive an opponent's secure effectively scoring 2 victory points for doing so.

If you face Kingpin's criminal syndicate on this crisis it might be worth doubling up on your central secure so that they can't just send 1 character like lizard or black cat at the end of round 1 to steal it off you.

The crisis does damage to characters in the cleanup phase if 3 or more characters are within 1.

Survivors Search for Safe Shelter (20)

Survivors Search for Safe Shelter Credit Atomic Mass Games

Max 3 VPs - King of the Hill

Another new asymmetrical secure with one central point and two opposite one another on the other flank. The gimmick of this crisis is that if another ally is contesting the same objective they can pay a power to add defence dice to an ally.

Adding defence die tends to be one of the worst defensive abilities, but considering this crisis is low scoring and fairly compact it could give the edge in a brawl.

With only 3 victory points available, teams that want to slow the game down will certainly like this crisis. When this is combined with a source extract then there will be no way to avoid a central fight.

Assault Ships Make Sweeping Search! (16)

Assault Ships Make Sweeping Search Credit Atomic Mass Games

Max 4 VPs - King of the Hill

This one has a very similar shape to the new Surprise assault Mutant Homes Destroyed extract with 4 secures staggered across the center.

There are 4 victory points available here and the crisis gives a once per turn reroll when attacking someone within 2 of the secure.

Being centrally focused with an attacking reroll once again encourages an aggressive game plan here.

When combined with the other Surprise assault extract then teams that can bunker up with strong turrets would do well here, standing on the points, picking up source extracts and shooting between them.

Overall Thoughts

It appears that this crisis rotation might be much brawlier than the previous set. Source extracts stop teams from picking up and running into a corner and the shapes of the new crisis are fairly central.

In addition in order to consistently pick up source extracts your characters will have to pay to interact with them every round.

With other extract types, once a character is holding one they no longer have to keep paying for them. With source extracts there's a power tax every round that some teams may struggle with if they also want to keep the rest of their kit online.

There are more asymmetrical shapes available for new crisis which is something we haven't seen before. This could have interesting implications for deployment as you may need to focus your threats on one side of the board over the other. It also may give more incentive to actually choose board edge as terrain brings a bigger factor when the crisis itself wants the game to be played in a certain area of the board.

Almost all the new crisis seem favorable for an attrition game plan. It will be interesting to see which of the existing crisis stay in the new rotation as that may dictate the type of games we see moving forward. Additionally we know there is a big shake up of health pools on the way which could make a big difference on how these missions actually play out.

When we get a full list of the upcoming crisis changes Tabletop Battles will do more coverage of how this might affect the meta moving forward.
